# DAIRY-CALIBRATION

## Target anchors
- Greek Yogurt → S
- Cheddar Cheese → B
- Ice Cream → D

## Current calibration strategy
- create a dairy-specific ruleset from scratch
- anchor the category around one definite S, one definite middle, and one definite D
- make protein usefulness and calcium identity the main positives, while sugar and saturated fat become the main negatives

## Why these anchors
- **Greek yogurt** is the clearest elite dairy anchor because it combines strong protein usefulness with a relatively clean overall dairy profile
- **Cheddar cheese** is the right middle reference because it is undeniably strong in protein and calcium, but the saturated fat and energy density hold it back
- **Ice cream** makes a clean D anchor because it is still recognisably dairy but performs weakly on the exact dairy traits the category is supposed to reward

## First-pass dairy philosophy
- protein usefulness should matter a lot
- calcium and supportive minerals should matter a lot
- sugar and saturated fat should be major penalties
- cholesterol matters some, but not as much as the main two negatives
- fibre is not part of the category

## Active tier targets
This first pass aims for:
- Greek Yogurt → S
- Cheddar Cheese → B
- Ice Cream → D

If the ordering is wrong, change weights or bands.
If the ordering is right but tiers are compressed, change thresholds.
