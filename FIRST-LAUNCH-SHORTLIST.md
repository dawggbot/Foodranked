# FIRST-LAUNCH-SHORTLIST

## Goal

Seed the first FoodRanked batch with foods that are:
- instantly recognizable
- visually clear
- likely to provoke opinions
- spread across tiers instead of all feeling the same
- good for validating whether the format works, not just whether the nutrition logic works

## Recommended first 10

1. **Salmon**
   - clear premium-health signal
   - strong chance of a satisfying high-tier reveal

2. **Bacon**
   - extremely recognizable
   - emotionally sticky because people already debate it

3. **Oats**
   - staple food, broad relevance, strong test of the grain logic

4. **Rice Cakes**
   - perfect for the “healthy-looking but weak-tier” format

5. **Extra Virgin Olive Oil**
   - strong prestige food with likely discussion in comments

6. **Butter**
   - classic debate food and a great foil to olive oil later

7. **Raspberries**
   - likely strong fruit result with clean visual identity

8. **Banana**
   - universally familiar and useful for testing audience disagreement

9. **Kale**
   - obvious health-halo food that still needs to earn the verdict

10. **Regular Cola**
   - dead-simple D-tier anchor and highly legible to any viewer

## Why this mix works

This batch gives you:
- likely S/A winners: `salmon`, `extra-virgin-olive-oil`, `raspberries`, `kale`
- mid/debate foods: `oats`, `banana`, `butter`
- obvious weak/controversial foods: `bacon`, `rice-cakes`, `cola-regular`

That matters because the early goal is not just “generate episodes.”
The goal is to see whether the format feels fun across:
- obvious wins
- unexpected disappointments
- arguments in the middle
