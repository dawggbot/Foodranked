# FRUITS-CALIBRATION

## Target anchors
- Raspberries → S
- Banana → B
- Dates → D

## Current calibration strategy
- create a fruit-specific ruleset from scratch
- anchor the category around one definite S, one definite middle, and one definite D
- make sugar moderation the core fruit logic, with fibre and glycemic behaviour doing most of the separation work

## Why these anchors
- **Raspberries** are a clean elite fruit anchor because fibre moderates sweetness extremely well and the glycemic behaviour is gentle
- **Banana** is the right middle reference because it is practical and respectable, but clearly less moderated than the best fruits
- **Dates** make a very clean D anchor because the sugar density is extreme even though they remain a recognisable whole fruit

## First-pass fruit philosophy
- sugar must be judged in context, not by purity tests
- fibre should strongly reward fruits that moderate sweetness well
- glycemic behaviour should matter a lot
- vitamins and supportive minerals should help strong fruits separate upward
- fats and proteins should barely matter

## Active tier targets
This first pass aims for:
- Raspberries → S
- Banana → B
- Dates → D

If the ordering is wrong, change weights or bands.
If the ordering is right but tiers are compressed, change thresholds.
