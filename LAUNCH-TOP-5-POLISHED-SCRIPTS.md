These are first-pass creator-ready narration drafts for the strongest launch candidates.

Goals:
- keep the `SUBJECT ranked.` opener
- stay short-form friendly
- sound cleaner and more human than raw generator output
- keep verdicts direct and comment-worthy

## 1) Bacon

**Narration draft**

Bacon ranked.

Today we’re ranking Bacon as a meat, per 100g, so let’s see where it actually lands.

Fat is 42g, and this is where things start going wrong fast.

Carbs are basically irrelevant here.

Protein is 37g, and yes, the protein quality is strong.

B12 helps a bit, but the micronutrient case is nowhere near enough to save it.

The pros are obvious: it tastes great, it makes food more addictive, and people love it.

But the downsides are rough: the sodium is bad, the processing is bad, and the fat profile is bad for the category.

Overall, Bacon lands in D tier.

Bacon gets a 39 overall.

**Why this version is better**
- clearer turn from “people love it” to “but nutritionally it still loses” 
- more natural than listing abstract metric winners too literally

## 2) Rice Cakes

**Narration draft**

Rice Cakes ranked.

Today we’re ranking Rice Cakes as a grain, per 100g, so let’s see where they actually land.

Fat is only 3g, so this was never going to be a fat story.

Carbs are 81.5g, and this is where the problem shows up: high glycemic impact, not much payoff, and weak staying power.

Protein is only 7g, so that’s not rescuing anything either.

The pros are mostly practical: they’re portable, easy to carry, and easy to throw toppings on.

But the downsides are exactly why people argue about them: weak grain payoff, harsh glycemic impact, and not very filling for the calories.

Overall, Rice Cakes land in D tier.

Rice Cakes get a 21 overall.

**Why this version is better**
- stronger “healthy-looking but weak-tier” framing
- easier for viewers to follow than raw metric callouts

## 3) Extra Virgin Olive Oil

**Narration draft**

Extra Virgin Olive Oil ranked.

Today we’re ranking Extra Virgin Olive Oil as a fat, per 100g, so let’s see where it actually lands.

Fat is 100g, obviously, so this whole verdict comes down to fat quality.

And that’s exactly why olive oil scores so well here.

The vitamin support is stronger than most people expect, and the overall category fit is excellent.

The biggest pros are clear: strong fat profile, useful polyphenols, and a very clean ingredient identity.

The cons are still real: it’s extremely calorie-dense, quality varies a lot, and it’s not magic just because it has a health halo.

Overall, Extra Virgin Olive Oil lands in S tier.

It gets a 78 overall.

**Why this version is better**
- feels more confident and less robotic
- preserves trust by avoiding “olive oil worship” language

## 4) Regular Cola

**Narration draft**

Regular Cola ranked.

Today we’re ranking Regular Cola as a misc item, per 100g, so let’s see where it actually lands.

This one is simple.

There is basically no meaningful nutrition story here.

What matters is the context: it’s cheap, common, and easy to drink way too much of.

So even if it’s convenient and everywhere, the downside is doing almost all the talking.

Overall, Regular Cola lands in D tier.

## 5) Salmon

**Narration draft**

Salmon ranked.

Today we’re ranking Salmon as a meat, per 100g, so let’s see where it actually lands.

Fat is 13g, and in this case that is a very good thing, because the omega-3 support is doing serious work.

Carbs are zero, which doesn’t matter much here.

Protein is 20.4g, and the protein quality is excellent.

Then the micros push it even higher: B12 is huge, vitamin D is huge, and the overall package is just strong.

The pros are exactly what people expect: salmon has a great omega-3 reputation, it’s satisfying, and it brings real nutrient density.

The downsides are mostly practical: it can be expensive, sourcing matters, and storage matters.

Overall, Salmon lands in S tier.

It gets an 81 overall.

**Why this version is better**
- strong trust-builder episode
- more natural emphasis hierarchy
- keeps the “why” of the S tier clear
