# LEGUMES-CALIBRATION

## Target anchors
- Lentils → S
- Chickpeas → B
- Baked Beans → D

## Current calibration strategy
- create a legume-specific ruleset from scratch
- anchor the category around one definite S, one definite middle, and one definite D
- make fibre + low glycemic behaviour + useful protein the dominant category logic

## Why these anchors
- **Lentils** are the cleanest classic legume winner: strong fibre, slow carbs, useful protein, solid mineral support
- **Chickpeas** are a believable middle reference: strong and respectable, but not the cleanest possible legume on every dimension
- **Baked beans** make a useful D anchor because the extra sugar and processed format clearly weaken the category fit while still keeping the food recognisably legume-based

## First-pass legume philosophy
- carbs matter most, but specifically slow-carb behaviour
- fibre should be one of the biggest separators in the category
- protein should matter a lot, but not enough to rescue sugary or heavily compromised legumes
- minerals should help distinguish strong staples from weaker ones
- fats are low-priority background signals only

## Active tier targets
This first pass aims for:
- Lentils → S
- Chickpeas → B
- Baked Beans → D

If the ordering is wrong, change weights or bands.
If the ordering is right but tiers are compressed, change thresholds.
