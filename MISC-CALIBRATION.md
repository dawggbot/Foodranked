# MISC-CALIBRATION

## Target anchors
- Green Tea → S
- Coffee → B
- Processed Honey → D

## Source-of-truth rule

This category follows the blueprint guidance:
- misc submacros do **not** get ranked
- vitamins and minerals get scored heavier
- pros/cons get scored **heaviest**
- philosophy: context-driven category — judged on effects, not composition

## Current calibration strategy
- ignore all submacros entirely for scoring
- let vitamins/minerals matter a bit
- let pros/cons dominate the final result far more than in any other category

## Why these anchors
- **Green tea** is the cleanest elite misc anchor because the value is mostly contextual upside with very little downside
- **Coffee** is the right middle reference because it has real upside but also obvious stimulant tradeoffs
- **Processed honey** is the weak anchor because it is mostly concentrated sugar and loses some of the case raw honey might have had

## Important note

This category is deliberately unlike the others.
It is not trying to compare composition-heavy whole foods.
It is trying to score low-macro, context-heavy ingestibles like:
- tea
- coffee
- honey
- seasonings
- similar edge-case items

## Active tier targets
This first pass aims for:
- Green Tea → S
- Coffee → B
- Processed Honey → D

If the ordering is wrong, change context scoring and thresholds first.
Do not try to force misc back into the normal macro/submacro model.
