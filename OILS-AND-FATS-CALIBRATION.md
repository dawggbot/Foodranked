# OILS-AND-FATS-CALIBRATION

## Target anchors
- Extra Virgin Olive Oil → S
- Butter → B
- Margarine → D

## Current calibration strategy
- create an oils-and-fats-specific ruleset from scratch
- anchor the category around one definite S, one definite middle, and one definite D
- keep fat chemistry as the dominant logic, but allow context to matter more here than in many other categories

## Why these anchors
- **Extra virgin olive oil** is the cleanest elite anchor because it has the strongest overall reputation and a very defensible fat-profile identity
- **Butter** is the intended middle anchor because, while flawed on chemistry, it still has a simple traditional-food identity and real-world legitimacy the user wants reflected
- **Margarine** makes the cleanest D anchor because processing, ingredient complexity, and formulation variability make it a weak trust/category fit even when some raw fat metrics look better than butter

## First-pass category philosophy
- fat chemistry is still king
- saturated fat and cholesterol matter a lot
- omega-3 and polyunsaturated fats matter a lot
- vitamins can help a bit
- context matters more than usual here because processing/tradition/ingredient simplicity are central to how people judge this category

## Active tier targets
This first pass aims for:
- Extra Virgin Olive Oil → S
- Butter → B
- Margarine → D

If the ordering is wrong, change weights or bands.
If the ordering is right but tiers are compressed, change thresholds.
