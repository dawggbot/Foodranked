# POST-PRESSURE-TEST-REVIEW

Quick stabilization decisions after the first cross-category pressure test.

## Tightened immediately

### Fruits
- **Problem:** Grapes landed in D, which looked harsher than intended for a common whole fruit.
- **Change:** widened the `C` band and narrowed the `D` band.
- **Reason:** keep clearly extreme sugar-density fruits like dates in D, but stop moderately flawed whole fruits from dropping too hard.

## Formalized rather than changed

### Oils & Fats
- Keep the current editorial model.
- This category is intentionally not science-neutral.
- It is a philosophy-driven category where processing/trust/tradition matter heavily.

### Meats
- Keep current thresholds for now.
- **Interpretation:** nutrient-dense organ meats like liver can rise high, but are not automatically elite.
- Treat current liver result as a feature, not a bug.

### Dairy
- Keep current scoring.
- **Interpretation:** multiple dairy archetypes can be S-tier if they genuinely earn it.
- Greek yogurt and cottage cheese both qualifying near the top is acceptable.

### Misc
- Keep current blueprint-driven model.
- Misc should remain effects/context-driven rather than composition-driven.
- Raw honey scoring above processed honey is the desired behavior.

## What still needs a true v2 later
- oils & fats
- fruits
- meats
- dairy
- misc

But the current system is stable enough to move on without pretending these categories are finished forever.
