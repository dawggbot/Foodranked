# TUBERS-CALIBRATION

## Target anchors
- Sweet Potato → S
- White Potato → B
- Fries → D

## Current calibration strategy
- create a tuber-specific ruleset from scratch
- anchor the category around one definite S, one definite middle, and one definite D
- make carb behaviour the dominant logic, with fibre and support micros helping separate cleaner tubers from rougher ones

## Why these anchors
- **Sweet potato** is the cleanest elite tuber anchor because it combines decent fibre, gentler GI, and standout vitamin support
- **White potato** is the right middle reference because it is practical and respectable, but not as clean or supportive as the best tubers
- **Fries** make the clearest D anchor because the fried format, rougher GI behaviour, and energy-density tradeoff are obvious category negatives

## First-pass tuber philosophy
- glycemic behaviour matters most
- fibre matters a lot
- vitamins and minerals should help strong wholefood tubers separate upward
- fats should stay low-priority except where preparation makes them a problem
- protein should barely matter

## Active tier targets
This first pass aims for:
- Sweet Potato → S
- White Potato → B
- Fries → D

If the ordering is wrong, change weights or bands.
If the ordering is right but tiers are compressed, change thresholds.
