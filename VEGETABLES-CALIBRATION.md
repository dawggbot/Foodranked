# VEGETABLES-CALIBRATION

## Target anchors
- Spinach → S
- Carrots → B
- Iceberg Lettuce → D

## Current calibration strategy
- create a vegetable-specific ruleset from scratch
- anchor the category around one definite S, one definite middle, and one definite D
- make micronutrient density the dominant logic, with fibre and low sugar/glycemic behaviour acting as major support signals

## Why these anchors
- **Spinach** is the clearest elite vegetable anchor because the micronutrient density is absurd and the sugar load is tiny
- **Carrots** are the right middle reference because they are clearly good and practical, but not as overwhelmingly dense as elite greens
- **Iceberg lettuce** makes a clean D anchor because the nutritional payoff is weak even though it is obviously still a vegetable

## First-pass vegetable philosophy
- vitamins and minerals should dominate the category
- fibre matters a lot
- low sugar and low glycemic behaviour should help distinguish stronger vegetables from sweeter/starchier ones
- fats and proteins should barely matter

## Active tier targets
This first pass aims for:
- Spinach → S
- Carrots → B
- Iceberg Lettuce → D

If the ordering is wrong, change weights or bands.
If the ordering is right but tiers are compressed, change thresholds.
