Apple ranked.

fat is 0.2g. omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. for a fruit, this really comes down to sugar control.

carbs is 13.8g. fibre is where things start to fall off. for fruit, the real question is whether the sweetness stays under control.

protein is 0.3g. bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. for a fruit, the cleanest wins are low sugar and decent fibre.

vitamin c at 5% DV, vitamin a at 1% DV stand out most. that is barely moving the needle.

minerals are basically not part of the case here.

on the plus side. hydrating whole-fruit package because water and whole-food structure make it easier to use well than juice or sweets. easy to use as a whole food because simple, recognisable, and realistic for everyday eating. brings a clean fruit identity because it still contributes something valuable even when it is not elite.

now the downsides. protein contribution is tiny because that is normal for fruit, but it still limits nutritional breadth. sugar can climb fast because a lot of fruits look healthy but still lean sweet. satiety is not especially strong because fruit often needs help from the rest of the meal.

Apple does have some solid upside, especially hydrating whole-fruit package and easy to use as a whole food. but protein contribution is tiny and sugar can climb fast keep it from climbing higher. best when you want sweetness that still earns its place nutritionally.

B tier.
