Apple ranked.

0.2g of fat. omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. for a fruit, this really comes down to sugar control.

13.8g of carbs. fibre is where things start to fall off. for fruit, the real question is whether the sweetness stays under control.

0.3g of protein. bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. for a fruit, the cleanest wins are low sugar and decent fibre.

the rest of the section is nothing special.

minerals are basically not part of the case here.

on the plus side: brings a clean fruit identity because it still contributes something valuable even when it is not elite. easy to use as a whole food because simple, recognisable, and realistic for everyday eating. usually beats ultra-processed sweet snacks because that replacement value matters in real life.

now the downsides: satiety is not especially strong because fruit often needs help from the rest of the meal. protein contribution is tiny because that is normal for fruit, but it still limits nutritional breadth. easy to eat quickly when it is very sweet because that can blur the line between light and sugar-heavy portions.

Apple does have some solid upside. but the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. best when you want sweetness that still earns its place nutritionally.

B tier.
