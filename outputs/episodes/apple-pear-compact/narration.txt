Pear ranked.

0.1g of fat. omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. for a fruit, you want sweetness to be kept in check.

15.2g of carbs. glycemic index, fibre, and sugar are doing most of the work. for fruit, the real question is whether the sweetness stays under control.

0.4g of protein. bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. for a fruit, sugar moderation is the whole game.

the rest is not doing much.

minerals are basically not part of the case here.

the upsides first: hydrating whole-fruit package because water and whole-food structure make it easier to use well than juice or sweets. easy to use as a whole food because simple, recognisable, and realistic for everyday eating. brings a clean fruit identity because it still contributes something valuable even when it is not elite.

on the weaker side: protein contribution is tiny because that is normal for fruit, but it still limits nutritional breadth. sugar can climb fast because a lot of fruits look healthy but still lean sweet. satiety is not especially strong because fruit often needs help from the rest of the meal.

Pear does have some solid upside. but the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. best when you want sweetness that still earns its place nutritionally.

B tier.
