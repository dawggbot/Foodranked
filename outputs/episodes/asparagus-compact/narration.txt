Asparagus!

-

Ranked!

-

0.1g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a vegetable, you want real micronutrient value, not just green vibes.

-

3.9g of carbs. Glycemic index, sugar, and fibre are doing most of the work. For vegetables, low downside is useful, but there still needs to be real payoff.

-

2.2g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. Protein is not the main pitch here, but extra support still matters.

-

Vitamin k at 35% daily value, vitamin a at 15% daily value stand out most. That is only mild support overall.

-

Minerals are basically not part of the case here.

-

Pros first: makes meals feel more balanced. The category has real value beyond single-nutrient stats. Very low downside whole food. Vegetables win a lot simply by offering clean nutritional payoff. Good volume for the calories. That matters for satiety and general usefulness.

-

On the weaker side: energy density is very low. Good for some use cases, but not enough on its own. Usually needs pairing to make a full meal. Most vegetables are not complete by themselves. Freshness and prep can matter. Practical friction is still a real downside.

-

Asparagus does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best when you want low downside and easy micronutrient support.

B tier.
