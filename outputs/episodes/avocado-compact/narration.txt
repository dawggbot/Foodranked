Avocado!

-

Ranked!

-

14.7g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a fruit, sugar moderation is the whole game.

-

8.5g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For fruit, the real question is whether the sweetness stays under control.

-

2g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. For a fruit, you want sweetness to be kept in check.

-

Vitamin c at 11% daily value stands out most. That is barely moving the needle.

-

Potassium at 10% daily value stands out most. Useful, but still pretty limited.

-

On the plus side: a true outlier for fruit fat quality. This behaves nothing like typical sweet fruit and that makes the category result exceptional. Easy to use as a whole food. Simple, recognisable, and realistic for everyday eating. Brings a clean fruit identity. It still contributes something valuable even when it is not elite.

-

The drawbacks next: calorie density is much higher than most fruits. Still useful, but it changes how casually you can throw it in. Can be easy to waste if timing is off. Perishability is a practical downside people actually feel. Protein contribution is tiny. That is normal for fruit, but it still limits nutritional breadth.

-

Avocado has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when you want sweetness that still earns its place nutritionally.

S tier.
