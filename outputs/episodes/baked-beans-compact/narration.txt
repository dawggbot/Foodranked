Baked Beans!

-

Ranked!

-

0.5g of fat. Saturated fat, omega3, and polyunsaturated fat are doing most of the work. For a legume, this is exactly the kind of profile you want.

-

27g of carbs. Glycemic index is where this starts to get messy. For legumes, the carbs look much better when fibre and protein are both backing them up.

-

5.5g of protein. Essential amino acids score is where things start to fall off. Protein support is a big part of what makes legumes worth it.

-

The rest is not doing much.

-

Minerals are basically not part of the case here.

-

On the plus side: cheap and convenient. Very easy to use as a quick meal component. Still keeps some fibre. Not completely nutritionally empty despite the weaker category fit. Has practical comfort-food appeal. People actually eat it, which matters in the real world.

-

The drawbacks next: added sugar is a major category problem. Legumes are supposed to win on slow-carb behaviour, and this pushes the opposite way. Processing drags the category fit down. The canned sweet-sauce format makes it a worse version of what legumes are supposed to be. Weak protein usefulness for a legume. It does not deliver the strong fibre-plus-protein combo that defines elite legumes.

-

Baked Beans does have a few real-world upsides. But the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. Best only when convenience outweighs the weaker fibre or protein payoff.

-

D tier.
