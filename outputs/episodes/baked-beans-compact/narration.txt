Baked Beans ranked.

0.5g of fat. saturated fat, omega3, and polyunsaturated fat are doing most of the work. for a legume, this is exactly the kind of profile you want.

27g of carbs. glycemic index is where this starts to get messy. for legumes, the carbs look much better when fibre and protein are both backing them up.

5.5g of protein. essential amino acids score is where things start to fall off. protein support is a big part of what makes legumes worth it.

the rest is not doing much.

minerals are basically not part of the case here.

on the plus side: cheap and convenient because very easy to use as a quick meal component. still keeps some fibre because not completely nutritionally empty despite the weaker category fit. has practical comfort-food appeal because people actually eat it, which matters in the real world.

the drawbacks next: added sugar is a major category problem because legumes are supposed to win on slow-carb behaviour, and this pushes the opposite way. processing drags the category fit down because the canned sweet-sauce format makes it a worse version of what legumes are supposed to be. weak protein usefulness for a legume because it does not deliver the strong fibre-plus-protein combo that defines elite legumes.

Baked Beans does have a few real-world upsides. but the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. best only when convenience outweighs the weaker fibre or protein payoff.

D tier.
