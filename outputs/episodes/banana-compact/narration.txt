Ranked!

0.3g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a fruit, you want sweetness to be kept in check.

-

22.8g of carbs. The sugar load matters more than you would want. For fruit, the real question is whether the sweetness stays under control.

-

1.1g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. For a fruit, sugar moderation is the whole game.

-

Vitamin c at 10% daily value stands out most. That is barely moving the needle.

-

Minerals are basically not part of the case here.

-

The upsides first: very practical everyday fruit. Cheap, portable, and easy to use, which matters for a fruit people actually eat often. Often easy to tolerate. A practical plus for many people compared with harsher foods. Brings a useful potassium identity. Not enough to make it elite, but still a real supportive strength.

-

The drawbacks next: sugar load is fairly high. Less moderated than the best fruits. Fibre is only modest. Decent, but not category-leading. Glycemic behaviour is only middling. Not harsh, just not one of the cleanest fruits on this axis.

-

Banana does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best when you want sweetness that still earns its place nutritionally.

B tier.
