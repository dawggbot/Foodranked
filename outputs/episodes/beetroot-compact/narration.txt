Beetroot!

-

Ranked!

-

0.2g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a tuber, the cleanest win is a more stable carb profile.

-

9.6g of carbs. Glycemic index is where this starts to get messy. For tubers, the carb side decides whether the food feels stable or flimsy.

-

1.6g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. Protein is usually limited here, so the other sections have to carry more.

-

Everything else is lackluster.

-

Minerals are basically not part of the case here.

-

The upsides first: practical staple carb source. Tubers are often judged by how usable they are day to day. Can be quite filling. A useful practical strength when the preparation stays sensible. Whole-food carb package. That matters because it is a cleaner baseline than heavily refined carbs.

-

Now the downsides: still a carb-heavy category. That is the core ceiling tubers have to manage. Satiety payoff depends a lot on format. Some versions are much steadier than others. Can go downhill fast when heavily processed. The category has a big preparation trap.

-

Beetroot does have a few real-world upsides. But the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. Best only when you want easy carbs and can accept the missing upside.

-

D tier.
