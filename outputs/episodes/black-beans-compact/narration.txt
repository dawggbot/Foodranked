Black Beans!

-

Ranked!

-

0.9g of fat. Saturated fat, polyunsaturated fat, and omega3 are doing most of the work. For a legume, stable carbs plus protein is the dream.

-

63.3g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For legumes, the carbs look much better when fibre and protein are both backing them up.

-

21.6g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein support is a big part of what makes legumes worth it.

-

The rest is pretty lackluster.

-

Magnesium at 30% daily value, iron at 29% daily value, potassium at 32% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

-

The upsides first: cheap staple with real payoff. This matters because a food is stronger when people can actually use it often. Filling for the calories. Legumes usually earn a lot of practical value from satiety. One of the cheaper ways to buy useful nutrition. That matters for real diets, not just theory.

-

Now the downsides: can take planning or prep. That is a small but real friction cost. Prep time can still be a barrier. Dry legumes especially ask more from the cook. Texture is not universally loved. That can reduce how often people actually use them.

-

Black Beans has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when you want fibre, protein, and actual meal utility together.

-

S tier.
