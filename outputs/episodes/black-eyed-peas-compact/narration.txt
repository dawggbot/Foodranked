The upsides first: does a lot of jobs at once. Protein, fibre, and meal utility are why legumes stay valuable. Cheap staple with real payoff. This matters because a food is stronger when people can actually use it often. Filling for the calories. Legumes usually earn a lot of practical value from satiety.

-

On the weaker side: can take planning or prep. That is a small but real friction cost. Prep time can still be a barrier. Dry legumes especially ask more from the cook. Texture is not universally loved. That can reduce how often people actually use them.

-

Black-Eyed Peas does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best when you want fibre, protein, and actual meal utility together.
