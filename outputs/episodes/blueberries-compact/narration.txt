Blueberries!

-

Ranked!

-

0.3g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a fruit, this really comes down to sugar control.

-

14.5g of carbs. Fibre is where things start to fall off. For fruit, the real question is whether the sweetness stays under control.

-

0.7g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. For a fruit, the cleanest wins are low sugar and decent fibre.

-

Vitamin c at 11% daily value stands out most. That is barely moving the needle.

-

Minerals are basically not part of the case here.

-

On the plus side: easy to use as a whole food. Simple, recognisable, and realistic for everyday eating. Brings a clean fruit identity. It still contributes something valuable even when it is not elite. Hydrating whole-fruit package. Water and whole-food structure make it easier to use well than juice or sweets.

-

Now the downsides: protein contribution is tiny. That is normal for fruit, but it still limits nutritional breadth. Satiety is not especially strong. Fruit often needs help from the rest of the meal. Sugar can climb fast. A lot of fruits look healthy but still lean sweet.

-

Blueberries does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best when you want sweetness that still earns its place nutritionally.

-

B tier.
