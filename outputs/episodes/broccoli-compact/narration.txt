Broccoli!

-

Ranked!

-

0.4g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a vegetable, clean nutrient density is the real test.

-

6.6g of carbs. Glycemic index, sugar, and fibre are doing most of the work. For vegetables, low downside is useful, but there still needs to be real payoff.

-

2.8g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. Protein is not the main pitch here, but extra support still matters.

-

Vitamin k at 85% daily value, vitamin c at 99% daily value, vitamin a at 12% daily value stand out most. That is major vitamin support.

-

Minerals are basically not part of the case here.

-

On the plus side: very strong micronutrient payoff. Likely to challenge spinach for elite vegetable status. Good fibre support. Solid maintenance-and-repair fit. Strong cruciferous reputation. Viewed as a flagship healthy vegetable.

-

The drawbacks next: taste can be polarising. Not universally loved. Prep matters a lot. Overcooking hurts it badly. Can be rough on digestion for some. A practical downside.

-

Broccoli has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when you want low downside and easy micronutrient support.

-

A tier.
