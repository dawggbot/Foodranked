Brown Rice ranked.

fat is 2.9g. omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. fat is basically not the story here.

carbs is 77.2g. glycemic index is where this starts to get messy. for grains, the carb quality matters much more than the raw number.

protein is 7.9g. bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. protein helps, but not enough if the carb side is weak.

vitamin e at 1% DV stand out most. that is barely moving the needle.

magnesium at 35% DV, zinc at 13% DV, iron at 8% DV stand out most. useful, but still pretty limited.

the upsides first. easy staple to build meals around because very practical in everyday meal planning. better category fit than white rice because at least retains more fibre and micronutrients than more refined versions. often easy for people to tolerate because a practical plus in some contexts.

on the weaker side. fibre is only moderate for a strong grain because not terrible, just not category-leading. glycemic behaviour is only okay because not one of the best grains on this dimension. micronutrient payoff is fairly average because does not stand out strongly in vitamins/minerals.

Brown Rice does have some solid upside, especially easy staple to build meals around and better category fit than white rice. but fibre is only moderate for a strong grain and glycemic behaviour is only okay keep it from climbing higher. best when you want a staple carb that actually brings something useful with it.

B tier.
