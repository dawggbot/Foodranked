Brown Rice!

-

Ranked!

-

2.9g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. Fat is basically not the story here.

-

77.2g of carbs. Glycemic index is where this starts to get messy. For grains, the carb quality matters much more than the raw number.

-

7.9g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein helps, but not enough if the carb side is weak.

-

Everything else is fairly underwhelming.

-

Magnesium at 35% daily value, zinc at 13% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

-

The upsides first: easy staple to build meals around. Very practical in everyday meal planning. Better category fit than white rice. At least retains more fibre and micronutrients than more refined versions. Often easy for people to tolerate. A practical plus in some contexts.

-

On the weaker side: fibre is only moderate for a strong grain. Not terrible, just not category-leading. Glycemic behaviour is only okay. Not one of the best grains on this dimension. Micronutrient payoff is fairly average. Does not stand out strongly in vitamins/minerals.

-

Brown Rice does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best when you want a staple carb that actually brings something useful with it.

-

B tier.
