Buckwheat!

-

Ranked!

-

3.4g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. Fat is basically not the story here.

-

71.5g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For grains, the carb quality matters much more than the raw number.

-

13.3g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein helps, but not enough if the carb side is weak.

-

Everything else is lackluster.

-

Magnesium at 55% daily value, iron at 12% daily value, zinc at 20% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

-

The upsides first: useful staple energy source. That matters because grains are often judged by how practical they are in real diets. Easy to batch cook at scale. That practicality is part of why grains matter. Works as a meal anchor. A lot of value comes from being easy to build around.

-

Now the downsides: quality can drop fast with processing. The grain category punishes refinement pretty hard. Needs strong pairings to feel complete. The grain rarely finishes the nutrition job alone. Protein quality is limited. Even the better grains usually need pairing.

-

Buckwheat has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when you want a staple carb that actually brings something useful with it.

-

A tier.
