Ranked!

1.3g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. Fat is basically not the story here.

-

75.9g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For grains, the carb quality matters much more than the raw number.

-

12.3g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein helps, but not enough if the carb side is weak.

-

Everything else here is pretty flat.

-

Magnesium at 41% daily value, iron at 14% daily value, zinc at 13% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

-

On the plus side: usually affordable and scalable. Practicality matters for staple foods. Useful staple energy source. That matters because grains are often judged by how practical they are in real diets. Works as a meal anchor. A lot of value comes from being easy to build around.

-

The drawbacks next: quality can drop fast with processing. The grain category punishes refinement pretty hard. Easy to overshoot portions. Staple carbs can pile up fast without much friction. Carb load is still the main burden. That is the category risk no grain fully escapes.

-

Bulgur has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when you want a staple carb that actually brings something useful with it.
