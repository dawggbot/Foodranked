Ranked!

-

81.1g of fat. Saturated fat is a major pressure point. For this category, the real question is fat quality.

-

0.1g of carbs. Outside of that, the profile is pretty quiet. Not relevant here.

-

0.9g of protein. Everything else is fairly underwhelming. Also not relevant here.

-

Vitamin e at 11% daily value, vitamin a at 28% daily value stand out most. That is barely moving the needle.

-

Minerals are basically not adding much here.

-

Pros first: simple traditional fat. This gives butter a cleaner identity than heavily processed fat substitutes. Very strong culinary usefulness. Butter has real kitchen legitimacy and practical use that matters in this category. Can fit as a moderate traditional fat. The editorial position here is that butter belongs as a flawed but legitimate middle-ground fat rather than a bottom-tier industrial product.

-

Now the downsides: saturated fat is a major drawback. This is the main reason butter cannot be an elite oils-and-fats food. Cholesterol is high. A second meaningful strike against the fat profile. Fat profile is clearly weaker than top-tier oils. Butter is not competing with the best oils on chemistry alone.

-

Butter does have a few real-world upsides. But the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. Best only in small amounts when the cooking job matters more than the nutrition case.

D tier.
