Carrots!

-

Ranked!

-

0.2g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a vegetable, nutrient payoff is the whole point.

-

9.6g of carbs. The sugar load matters more than you would want. For vegetables, low downside is useful, but there still needs to be real payoff.

-

0.9g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. Protein is not the main pitch here, but extra support still matters.

-

Vitamin a at 93% daily value, vitamin k at 11% daily value stand out most. That gives this food real vitamin support.

-

Minerals are basically not part of the case here.

-

On the plus side: very strong vitamin A identity. This is a major category strength and part of what makes carrots a genuinely good vegetable. Cheap and practical. Easy to buy, store, snack on, and cook with. Easy raw-snack use. A practical advantage compared with fussier vegetables.

-

On the weaker side: sweetness is a bit higher than the cleanest vegetables. Still fine, just not ultra-low-sugar by category standards. Micronutrient spread is narrower than elite greens. Great at some things, less absurdly dense overall than the very top vegetables. Protein is basically irrelevant. Not a real flaw, but still nearly absent.

-

Carrots does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best when you want low downside and easy micronutrient support.

-

B tier.
