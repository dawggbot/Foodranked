Carrots ranked.

fat is 0.2g. omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. for a vegetable, nutrient payoff is the whole point.

carbs is 9.6g. the sugar load matters more than you would want. for vegetables, low downside is useful, but there still needs to be real payoff.

protein is 0.9g. bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. protein is not the main pitch here, but extra support still matters.

vitamin a at 93% DV, vitamin k at 11% DV, vitamin c at 7% DV stand out most. that is only mild support overall.

minerals are basically not part of the case here.

on the plus side. very strong vitamin A identity because this is a major category strength and part of what makes carrots a genuinely good vegetable. cheap and practical because easy to buy, store, snack on, and cook with. easy raw-snack use because a practical advantage compared with fussier vegetables.

on the weaker side. sweetness is a bit higher than the cleanest vegetables because still fine, just not ultra-low-sugar by category standards. micronutrient spread is narrower than elite greens because great at some things, less absurdly dense overall than the very top vegetables. protein is basically irrelevant because not a real flaw, but still nearly absent.

Carrots does have some solid upside, especially very strong vitamin A identity and cheap and practical. but sweetness is a bit higher than the cleanest vegetables and micronutrient spread is narrower than elite greens keep it from climbing higher. best when you want low downside and easy micronutrient support.

B tier.
