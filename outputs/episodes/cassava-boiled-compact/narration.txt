Cassava (Boiled)!

-

Ranked!

-

0.4g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a tuber, glycemic behaviour matters a ton.

-

27.8g of carbs. Glycemic index is where this starts to get messy. For tubers, the carb side decides whether the food feels stable or flimsy.

-

1.4g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. Protein is usually limited here, so the other sections have to carry more.

-

Vitamin c at 17% daily value stands out most. That is barely moving the needle.

-

Minerals are basically not part of the case here.

-

The upsides first: whole-food carb package. That matters because it is a cleaner baseline than heavily refined carbs. Works across a lot of cooking styles. Utility counts for staple carbs. Practical staple carb source. Tubers are often judged by how usable they are day to day.

-

On the weaker side: still a carb-heavy category. That is the core ceiling tubers have to manage. Protein support is weak. Tubers still need help from the rest of the meal. Satiety payoff depends a lot on format. Some versions are much steadier than others.

-

Cassava (Boiled) does have a few real-world upsides. But the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. Best only when you want easy carbs and can accept the missing upside.

D tier.
