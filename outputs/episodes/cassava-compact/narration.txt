Cassava!

Ranked!

-

0.3g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a tuber, glycemic behaviour matters a ton.

-

38.1g of carbs. Glycemic index is where this starts to get messy. For tubers, the carb side decides whether the food feels stable or flimsy.

-

1.4g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. Protein is usually limited here, so the other sections have to carry more.

-

Vitamin c at 34% daily value stands out most. That is only mild support overall.

-

Minerals are basically not part of the case here.

-

On the plus side: major staple-energy role globally. Tests whether practical staple legitimacy can rescue rough carb behaviour. Very versatile staple. Broad use cases. Does bring some vitamin C. Not empty on micros.

-

Now the downsides: glycemic behaviour is brutal. A direct stress test for the tuber ruleset. Fibre payoff is weak. Poor for a stronger tuber. Preparation matters for safety. Not a trivial downside.

-

Cassava does have a few real-world upsides. But the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. Best only when you want easy carbs and can accept the missing upside.

D tier.
