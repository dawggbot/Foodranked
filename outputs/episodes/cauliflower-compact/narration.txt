Cauliflower!

-

Ranked!

-

0.3g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a vegetable, this is all about actual nutritional payoff.

-

5g of carbs. Glycemic index, sugar, and fibre are doing most of the work. For vegetables, low downside is useful, but there still needs to be real payoff.

-

1.9g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. Protein is not the main pitch here, but extra support still matters.

-

Vitamin c at 54% daily value, vitamin k at 13% daily value stand out most. That is only mild support overall.

-

Minerals are basically not part of the case here.

-

The upsides first: good volume for the calories. That matters for satiety and general usefulness. Helps add volume without much calorie cost. That makes vegetables easier to use often. Easy to build meals around. A practical plus because good foods need realistic use cases.

-

The drawbacks next: taste and texture can limit compliance. A strong paper profile still needs real-world repeatability. Usually needs pairing to make a full meal. Most vegetables are not complete by themselves. Rarely carries a meal by itself. That is normal here, but still part of the tradeoff.

-

Cauliflower does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best when you want low downside and easy micronutrient support.

-

B tier.
