Chestnuts!

-

Ranked!

-

2.2g of fat. Saturated fat, polyunsaturated fat, and omega3 are doing most of the work. For nuts, fat quality has to justify the calorie density.

-

45.5g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For a nut, fat quality matters a ton.

-

2.4g of protein. Essential amino acids score is where things start to fall off. Protein is support here, not the whole argument.

-

Outside of that, the profile is pretty quiet.

-

Minerals are basically not part of the case here.

-

On the plus side: has strong seasonal appeal. Could be useful for content packaging even if the nutrition fit is weaker. Lighter than most nuts. This can be practically appealing to some viewers. Works in both sweet and savoury uses. Useful culinary flexibility.

-

Now the downsides: poor fit for the nut category goals. Very weak on the fat-quality signals that define stronger nuts. More starch-heavy than ideal here. Moves away from what the nut category is supposed to reward. Protein support is weak. Does not offer much in the protein-quality lane either.

-

Chestnuts does have a few real-world upsides. But the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. Best only in small amounts when the calories stay under control.

-

D tier.
