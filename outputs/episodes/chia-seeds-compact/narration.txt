Chia Seeds!

-

Ranked!

-

30.7g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For seeds, the fat profile is one of the biggest reasons they earn their place.

-

42.1g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For a seed, the best ones punch way above their size.

-

16.5g of protein. Essential amino acid support is one of the better parts here. Protein is a bonus, but not enough by itself.

-

Everything else is kind of whatever.

-

Magnesium at 80% daily value, zinc at 42% daily value, iron at 43% daily value stand out most. That gives this food real mineral support.

-

The upsides first: extreme fibre density. Adds standout digestive and satiety value even beyond the headline nutrient display. Can be very filling for the amount used. The gel-forming fibre profile gives it practical fullness value. Easy to add into meals. Can be mixed into oats, yogurt, puddings, and smoothies without much friction.

-

On the weaker side: contains some anti-nutrients. Phytates may reduce mineral uptake somewhat. Texture can put some people off. The gel-like texture is not universally appealing. Usually works best with some preparation. Often needs soaking or mixing into something else to shine.

-

Chia Seeds has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best as a support food that boosts meals instead of carrying them alone.

-

S tier.
