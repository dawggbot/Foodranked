Chickpeas ranked.

6g of fat. omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. for a legume, this is exactly the kind of profile you want.

60.7g of carbs. fibre is doing a lot of the work here. for legumes, the carbs look much better when fibre and protein are both backing them up.

19.3g of protein. bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. protein support is a big part of what makes legumes worth it.

everything else here is pretty flat.

iron at 34% daily value, magnesium at 29% daily value, zinc at 27% daily value stand out most. useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

on the plus side: strong all-round legume profile because fibre, minerals, and useful protein all show up well enough to make it a very solid category food. easy to use across lots of meals because works in bowls, curries, salads, hummus, and snacks. pretty filling and steady because useful as a practical staple rather than just a niche health food.

now the downsides: sugar is a bit high for the category because not a disaster, but it is less clean than the best legumes on this axis. can be rough on some people digestively because legume tolerance varies a lot. contains some anti-nutrients because phytates may reduce mineral uptake somewhat.

Chickpeas has a genuinely strong overall case. the practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. best when you want fibre, protein, and actual meal utility together.

S tier.
