Chickpeas ranked.

fat is 6g. omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. for a legume, this is exactly the kind of profile you want.

carbs is 60.7g. fibre is doing a lot of the work here. for legumes, the carbs look much better when fibre and protein are both backing them up.

protein is 19.3g. essential amino acids score is where things start to fall off. protein support is a big part of what makes legumes worth it.

vitamin e at 4% DV, vitamin c at 7% DV stand out most. that is barely moving the needle.

iron at 34% DV, magnesium at 29% DV, zinc at 27% DV stand out most. useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

on the plus side. strong all-round legume profile because fibre, minerals, and useful protein all show up well enough to make it a very solid category food. easy to use across lots of meals because works in bowls, curries, salads, hummus, and snacks. pretty filling and steady because useful as a practical staple rather than just a niche health food.

now the downsides. sugar is a bit high for the category because not a disaster, but it is less clean than the best legumes on this axis. can be rough on some people digestively because legume tolerance varies a lot. contains some anti-nutrients because phytates may reduce mineral uptake somewhat.

Chickpeas does have some solid upside, especially strong all-round legume profile and easy to use across lots of meals. but sugar is a bit high for the category and can be rough on some people digestively keep it from climbing higher. best when you want fibre, protein, and actual meal utility together.

B tier.
