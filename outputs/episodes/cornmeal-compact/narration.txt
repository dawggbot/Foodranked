3.9g of fat. omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. fat is basically not the story here.

79g of carbs. fibre is doing a lot of the work here. for grains, the carb quality matters much more than the raw number.

7.1g of protein. bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. protein helps, but not enough if the carb side is weak.

the rest is pretty lackluster.

iron at 13% daily value, magnesium at 18% daily value, zinc at 12% daily value stand out most. useful, but still pretty limited.

the upsides first: useful staple energy source because that matters because grains are often judged by how practical they are in real diets. works as a meal anchor because a lot of value comes from being easy to build around. usually affordable and scalable because practicality matters for staple foods.

now the downsides: protein quality is limited because even the better grains usually need pairing. carb load is still the main burden because that is the category risk no grain fully escapes. quality can drop fast with processing because the grain category punishes refinement pretty hard.

Cornmeal does have some solid upside. but the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. best when you want a staple carb that actually brings something useful with it.

B tier.
