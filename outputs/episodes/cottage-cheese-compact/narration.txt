Cottage Cheese!

Ranked!

-

4.3g of fat. Saturated fat, cholesterol, and polyunsaturated fat are doing most of the work. For dairy, the fat side can either add richness or drag the whole thing down.

-

3.4g of carbs. Sugar and glycemic index matter most here. For dairy, the best versions keep the convenience without wrecking the fat profile.

-

11.1g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Useful protein can rescue a lot of weaker traits here.

-

Vitamin b12 at 13% daily value stands out most. That is barely moving the needle.

-

Minerals are basically not part of the case here.

-

On the plus side: lean high-protein dairy. Tests whether greek yogurt is the only clean dairy S. Very filling. Strong practical upside. Fairly simple food. Strong dairy identity without dessert drift.

-

The drawbacks next: can be fairly salty. Practical downside. Texture is divisive. Not universally liked. Not suitable for everyone. Tolerance varies.

-

Cottage Cheese has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when the protein payoff or fermentation angle outweighs the fat and sodium tradeoffs.
