Dates!

-

Ranked!

-

0.4g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a fruit, being sweet is normal, but controlling it is what matters.

-

75g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For fruit, the real question is whether the sweetness stays under control.

-

2.5g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. For a fruit, you want sweetness to be kept in check.

-

The rest is pretty lackluster.

-

Potassium at 15% daily value, magnesium at 11% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still pretty limited.

-

The upsides first: still brings some useful minerals. Not nutritionally empty despite the weak fruit fit. Can be useful in high-energy contexts. There are niche cases where the dense sugar load is useful. Still a recognisable whole fruit. That matters for fairness even when the category fit is weak.

-

On the weaker side: sugar density is a major category problem. Fruit is supposed to moderate sugar with fibre and payoff, and dates push the sugar side extremely hard. Glycemic behaviour is rough for fruit. Much weaker fit than fruits with gentler carb behaviour. Dried-fruit concentration makes the sugar hit even harsher. This is the cleanest reason dates should remain below ordinary whole fruits like grapes.

-

Dates does have a few real-world upsides. But the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. Best only when the convenience or taste matters more than the nutrition return.

-

C tier.
