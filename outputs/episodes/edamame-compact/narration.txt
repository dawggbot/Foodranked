Edamame!

-

Ranked!

-

5.2g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a legume, fibre and practicality are supposed to carry the case.

-

8.9g of carbs. Glycemic index, fibre, and sugar are doing most of the work. For legumes, the carbs look much better when fibre and protein are both backing them up.

-

11.9g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein support is a big part of what makes legumes worth it.

-

Vitamin c at 10% daily value stands out most. That is barely moving the needle.

-

Iron at 15% daily value, magnesium at 16% daily value, zinc at 12% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still pretty limited.

-

Pros first: one of the cheaper ways to buy useful nutrition. That matters for real diets, not just theory. Filling for the calories. Legumes usually earn a lot of practical value from satiety. Cheap staple with real payoff. This matters because a food is stronger when people can actually use it often.

-

Now the downsides: can be rough on digestion for some people. Practical tolerance still matters. Can take planning or prep. That is a small but real friction cost. Prep time can still be a barrier. Dry legumes especially ask more from the cook.

-

Edamame does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best when you want fibre, protein, and actual meal utility together.

-

B tier.
