The upsides first: polyphenol reputation is a real plus. It brings a widely recognised antioxidant angle beyond the visible macro profile. Contains useful polyphenols. Adds value beyond the core fat chemistry alone. Very simple ingredient identity. A clear wholefood-adjacent advantage over more industrial fat products.

-

On the weaker side: extremely energy dense. Still pure fat, so calories accumulate fast. Quality varies a lot. Source and authenticity matter in practice. Easy to overpour without noticing. A healthy image can make portion creep happen fast in practice.

-

Extra Virgin Olive Oil has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best in controlled amounts where fat quality is the main reason to use it.
