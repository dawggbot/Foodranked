Feta!

-

Ranked!

-

21.3g of fat. Saturated fat is a major pressure point. For dairy, the fat side can either add richness or drag the whole thing down.

-

4.1g of carbs. Sugar and glycemic index matter most here. For a dairy food, this is doing the right stuff.

-

14.2g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Useful protein can rescue a lot of weaker traits here.

-

Vitamin b12 at 27% daily value, vitamin a at 14% daily value stand out most. That is only mild support overall.

-

Calcium at 37% daily value, zinc at 19% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

-

The upsides first: easy to fit into familiar meals. That lowers friction for regular use. Can be a very convenient protein vehicle. Convenience is one of dairy’s strongest practical advantages. Slots into meals very easily. Practical versatility matters a lot for dairy.

-

On the weaker side: fat quality varies wildly across dairy. Some dairy is clean, some really is not. Not everyone tolerates dairy well. That is a real use-case limitation. Processing changes the category a lot. The label alone does not tell the whole story.

-

Feta does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best when the protein payoff or fermentation angle outweighs the fat and sodium tradeoffs.

-

B tier.
