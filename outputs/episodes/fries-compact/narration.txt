Fries!

-

Ranked!

-

15g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a tuber, this mostly lives or dies on carb behaviour.

-

41g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For tubers, the carb side decides whether the food feels stable or flimsy.

-

3.4g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. Protein is usually limited here, so the other sections have to carry more.

-

The rest is pretty lackluster.

-

Potassium at 13% daily value stands out most. Useful, but still pretty limited.

-

Pros first: strong comfort-food appeal. A very real reason people like them, even if the nutrition fit is weak. Still retains some tuber identity. Not nutritionally empty, just much worse than stronger tuber forms. Satisfying texture and taste. Part of the real-world appeal, especially for content framing.

-

Now the downsides: fried format is a major category negative. This drags tubers away from the cleaner energy-food role the category is supposed to reward. Glycemic behaviour is rough. A major practical strike against the food in a carb-behaviour category. Energy density is much harsher than ideal. Much easier to overshoot calories than with cleaner tuber forms.

-

Fries does have a few real-world upsides. But the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. Best only when you want easy carbs and can accept the missing upside.

-

D tier.
