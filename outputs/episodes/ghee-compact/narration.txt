on the plus side: highly useful in cooking because practical culinary value matters here. efficient energy source because that can be a strength in the right context. helps meals actually work because a fat that people can use well matters more than a theoretical one.

on the weaker side: extremely calorie dense by default because that is the unavoidable category burden. easy to overpour without noticing because practical portion creep is real. not a complete food on its own because even the better fats are still just part of a meal.

Ghee does have a few real-world upsides. but the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. best only in small amounts when the cooking job matters more than the nutrition case.
