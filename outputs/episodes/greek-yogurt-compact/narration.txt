Greek Yogurt!

-

Ranked!

-

5g of fat. Cholesterol, saturated fat, and omega3 are doing most of the work. For dairy, the fat side can either add richness or drag the whole thing down.

-

3.9g of carbs. Sugar and glycemic index matter most here. For dairy, that is exactly the kind of thing you want.

-

9g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Useful protein can rescue a lot of weaker traits here.

-

Vitamin b12 at 22% daily value stands out most. That is only mild support overall.

-

Calcium at 11% daily value stands out most. Useful, but still pretty limited.

-

On the plus side: fermented-food angle adds extra value. That gives it a practical benefit beyond the visible protein and fat numbers alone. Quite filling for the calories. The protein density gives it strong practical satiety value. Easy high-protein staple. Very easy to build meals or snacks around.

-

Now the downsides: saturated fat is not trivial. Still much cleaner than weak dairy foods, but not ultra-low-fat. Not suitable for everyone. Lactose tolerance and dairy tolerance vary. Healthiness can shift with toppings. Sugary add-ins can change the practical outcome fast.

-

Greek Yogurt has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when the protein payoff or fermentation angle outweighs the fat and sodium tradeoffs.

-

S tier.
