On the plus side: catechin content adds real upside. That gives it a useful compound story beyond the near-empty nutrition label. Strong polyphenol reputation. The core appeal of green tea lives in its compounds and effects rather than classic nutrition composition. Nearly zero-calorie daily use. A huge practical advantage in a category judged more by effect than macros.

-

On the weaker side: taste can be a barrier. Not everyone enjoys it enough to use consistently. Quality varies by source. The practical value depends somewhat on the product. Can be a bit fussier than grab-and-go drinks. A tiny practical downside, but not a serious one.

-

Green Tea has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best treated as a context item rather than a nutritional cornerstone.
