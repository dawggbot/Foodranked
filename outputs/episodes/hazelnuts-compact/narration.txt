Hazelnuts!

-

Ranked!

-

60.8g of fat. Saturated fat, omega3, and polyunsaturated fat are doing most of the work. For nuts, fat quality has to justify the calorie density.

-

16.7g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For a nut, you need enough upside to make the density worth it.

-

15g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein is support here, not the whole argument.

-

Vitamin e at 100% daily value stands out most. That is major vitamin support.

-

Magnesium at 41% daily value, zinc at 18% daily value, iron at 26% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

-

Pros first: still a recognisable whole-food option. That keeps them cleaner than most processed snacks. Holds up as a better snack default than most packaged options. That real-world swap value counts. Fat and texture help satiety. They can hold up better than many snack foods.

-

The drawbacks next: protein is useful but not elite. Nuts help, but they are not protein stars. Good versions can get expensive. That affects how realistic frequent use is. Portion control can get slippery fast. Dense foods punish casual handful math.

-

Hazelnuts does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best in small amounts where the fats and minerals matter more than sheer calories.

-

B tier.
