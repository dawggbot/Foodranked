Hemp Seeds!

-

Ranked!

-

48.8g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For seeds, the fat profile is one of the biggest reasons they earn their place.

-

8.7g of carbs. Fibre is where things start to fall off. For a seed, that is a huge win.

-

31.6g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein is a bonus, but not enough by itself.

-

Everything else here is pretty flat.

-

Magnesium at 175% daily value, zinc at 66% daily value, iron at 44% daily value stand out most. That is major mineral support.

-

The upsides first: usually brings decent fat support. That is one of the category strengths. Easy to sprinkle into meals for a real upgrade. Seeds often shine as support foods. Tiny food with serious nutrient density. Seeds often overperform for their size.

-

The drawbacks next: real serving sizes are often small. Some strong paper numbers are hard to cash out in practice. Paper scores can overstate realistic serving sizes. People often eat far less than 100 grams. Calorie density still stacks up fast. Tiny foods can still overshoot fast.

-

Hemp Seeds has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best as a support food that boosts meals instead of carrying them alone.

-

A tier.
