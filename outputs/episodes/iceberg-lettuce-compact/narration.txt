Iceberg Lettuce!

-

Ranked!

-

0.1g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a vegetable, the question is whether the payoff is worth the plate space.

-

3g of carbs. Glycemic index, fibre, and sugar are doing most of the work. For vegetables, low downside is useful, but there still needs to be real payoff.

-

0.9g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. Protein is not the main pitch here, but extra support still matters.

-

Vitamin k at 17% daily value stands out most. That is barely moving the needle.

-

Minerals are basically not part of the case here.

-

The upsides first: adds hydrating volume. It can bulk out meals with almost no calories. Easy salad base. Very practical in sandwiches, burgers, and salads. Mild and easy to tolerate. A practical plus for people who dislike bitter greens.

-

On the weaker side: micronutrient payoff is weak. That is a major problem in a category meant to reward nutrient density. Fibre payoff is weak for a vegetable. It does not offer much of the maintenance-and-repair value the category is supposed to reward. Mostly water with limited nutritional return. Very low payoff compared with stronger vegetables.

-

Iceberg Lettuce does have a few real-world upsides. But the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. Best only when you need a low-commitment add-on rather than a nutrient-dense anchor.

-

D tier.
