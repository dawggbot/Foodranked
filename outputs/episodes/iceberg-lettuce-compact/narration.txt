Iceberg Lettuce ranked.

0.1g of fat. omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. for a vegetable, the question is whether the payoff is worth the plate space.

3g of carbs. glycemic index, fibre, and sugar are doing most of the work. for vegetables, low downside is useful, but there still needs to be real payoff.

0.9g of protein. bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. protein is not the main pitch here, but extra support still matters.

vitamin k at 17% daily value, vitamin a at 4% daily value, vitamin c at 3% daily value stand out most. that is barely moving the needle.

minerals are basically not part of the case here.

the upsides first: adds hydrating volume because it can bulk out meals with almost no calories. easy salad base because very practical in sandwiches, burgers, and salads. mild and easy to tolerate because a practical plus for people who dislike bitter greens.

on the weaker side: micronutrient payoff is weak because that is a major problem in a category meant to reward nutrient density. fibre payoff is weak for a vegetable because it does not offer much of the maintenance-and-repair value the category is supposed to reward. mostly water with limited nutritional return because very low payoff compared with stronger vegetables.

Iceberg Lettuce does have a few real-world upsides. but the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. best only when you need a low-commitment add-on rather than a nutrient-dense anchor.

D tier.
