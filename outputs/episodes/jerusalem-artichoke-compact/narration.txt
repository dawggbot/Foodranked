Jerusalem Artichoke!

Ranked!

-

0g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a tuber, the question is whether the starch is behaving itself.

-

17.4g of carbs. Starch is doing most of the heavy lifting here. For tubers, the carb side decides whether the food feels stable or flimsy.

-

2g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. Protein is usually limited here, so the other sections have to carry more.

-

Outside of that, the profile is pretty quiet.

-

Minerals are basically not part of the case here.

-

Pros first: easy comfort-food base with less baggage than junk food. That practical swap value matters. Practical staple carb source. Tubers are often judged by how usable they are day to day. Whole-food carb package. That matters because it is a cleaner baseline than heavily refined carbs.

-

On the weaker side: satiety payoff depends a lot on format. Some versions are much steadier than others. Protein support is weak. Tubers still need help from the rest of the meal. Preparation changes outcomes a lot. What you do to a tuber can make or break it.

-

Jerusalem Artichoke does have a few real-world upsides. But the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. Best only when you want easy carbs and can accept the missing upside.

D tier.
