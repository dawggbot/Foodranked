Pros first: fermented dairy practicality is a real plus. It keeps the category strengths while usually being a bit more interesting in practice. Clear calcium identity. That is a core category strength. Easy protein delivery for normal diets. Dairy gets real credit for convenience.

-

On the weaker side: processing changes the category a lot. The label alone does not tell the whole story. Some versions get dense fast on fat, sodium, or both. The category swings a lot by format. Not everyone tolerates dairy well. That is a real use-case limitation.

-

Plain Kefir has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when the protein payoff or fermentation angle outweighs the fat and sodium tradeoffs.
