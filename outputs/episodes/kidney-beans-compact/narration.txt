Kidney Beans!

-

Ranked!

-

0.8g of fat. Saturated fat, polyunsaturated fat, and omega3 are doing most of the work. For a legume, fibre and practicality are supposed to carry the case.

-

60g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For legumes, the carbs look much better when fibre and protein are both backing them up.

-

23.6g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein support is a big part of what makes legumes worth it.

-

Everything else is kind of whatever.

-

Magnesium at 35% daily value, iron at 29% daily value, potassium at 29% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

-

On the plus side: one of the cheaper ways to buy useful nutrition. That matters for real diets, not just theory. Does a lot of jobs at once. Protein, fibre, and meal utility are why legumes stay valuable. Filling for the calories. Legumes usually earn a lot of practical value from satiety.

-

Now the downsides: texture is not universally loved. That can reduce how often people actually use them. Can take planning or prep. That is a small but real friction cost. Prep time can still be a barrier. Dry legumes especially ask more from the cook.

-

Kidney Beans has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when you want fibre, protein, and actual meal utility together.

-

S tier.
