Kiwi!

-

Ranked!

-

0.5g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a fruit, the cleanest wins are low sugar and decent fibre.

-

14.7g of carbs. Fibre is where things start to fall off. For fruit, the real question is whether the sweetness stays under control.

-

1.1g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. For a fruit, this really comes down to sugar control.

-

Vitamin c at 78% daily value stands out most. That is major vitamin support.

-

Minerals are basically not part of the case here.

-

The upsides first: brings a clean fruit identity. It still contributes something valuable even when it is not elite. Easy to use as a whole food. Simple, recognisable, and realistic for everyday eating. Usually beats ultra-processed sweet snacks. That replacement value matters in real life.

-

On the weaker side: can be easy to waste if timing is off. Perishability is a practical downside people actually feel. Protein contribution is tiny. That is normal for fruit, but it still limits nutritional breadth. Satiety is not especially strong. Fruit often needs help from the rest of the meal.

-

Kiwi has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when you want sweetness that still earns its place nutritionally.

A tier.
