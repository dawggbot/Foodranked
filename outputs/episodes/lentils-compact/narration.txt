Lentils ranked.

fat is 1.1g. saturated fat, polyunsaturated fat, and omega3 are doing most of the work. for a legume, this is exactly the kind of profile you want.

carbs is 60.1g. fibre is doing a lot of the work here. for legumes, the carbs look much better when fibre and protein are both backing them up.

protein is 25.8g. bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. protein support is a big part of what makes legumes worth it.

vitamin e at 3% DV, vitamin c at 8% DV stand out most. that is barely moving the needle.

iron at 37% DV, magnesium at 30% DV, zinc at 23% DV stand out most. useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

on the plus side: one of the best budget staple foods because it is unusually cheap, filling, and useful in real meals compared with a lot of healthy-food staples. very filling for the calories because the fibre and protein combo gives it strong practical fullness. usually affordable and practical because makes it easy to use as a real staple rather than a niche health food.

now the downsides: contains some anti-nutrients because phytates may reduce mineral uptake somewhat. can be rough on some people digestively because not everyone handles legumes equally well. needs some cooking effort because a small practical friction versus more convenient foods.

Lentils has a genuinely strong overall case, especially one of the best budget staple foods and very filling for the calories. the downsides are there, but contains some anti-nutrients and can be rough on some people digestively do not outweigh the upside. best when you want fibre, protein, and actual meal utility together.

S tier.
