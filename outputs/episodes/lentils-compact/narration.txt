Lentils ranked.

fat is 1.1g. saturated fat, polyunsaturated fat, and omega3 are doing most of the work. for a legume, this is exactly the kind of profile you want.

carbs is 60.1g. the fibre support is doing a lot of the work. for legumes, the carbs look much better when fibre and protein are both backing them up.

protein is 25.8g. protein quality is a real strength here. protein support is a big part of what makes legumes worth it.

vitamin e at 3% DV, vitamin c at 8% DV stand out most. that is barely moving the needle.

iron at 37% DV, magnesium at 30% DV, zinc at 23% DV stand out most. useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

on the plus side. nearly ideal legume category fit because strong fibre, slow carbs, and useful protein all line up with what the category is supposed to reward. very filling for the calories because the fibre and protein combo gives it strong practical fullness. usually affordable and practical because makes it easy to use as a real staple rather than a niche health food.

now the downsides. contains some anti-nutrients because phytates may reduce mineral uptake somewhat. can be rough on some people digestively because not everyone handles legumes equally well. needs some cooking effort because a small practical friction versus more convenient foods.

Lentils has a genuinely strong overall case, especially nearly ideal legume category fit and very filling for the calories. the downsides are there, but contains some anti-nutrients and can be rough on some people digestively do not outweigh the upside. best when you want fibre, protein, and actual meal utility together.

S tier.
