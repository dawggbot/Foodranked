Lentils!

-

Ranked!

-

1.1g of fat. Saturated fat, omega3, and polyunsaturated fat are doing most of the work. For a legume, this is exactly the kind of profile you want.

-

60.1g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For legumes, the carbs look much better when fibre and protein are both backing them up.

-

25.8g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein support is a big part of what makes legumes worth it.

-

Outside of that, the profile is pretty quiet.

-

Iron at 37% daily value, magnesium at 30% daily value, zinc at 23% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

-

On the plus side: one of the best budget staple foods. It is unusually cheap, filling, and useful in real meals compared with a lot of healthy-food staples. Very filling for the calories. The fibre and protein combo gives it strong practical fullness. Usually affordable and practical. Makes it easy to use as a real staple rather than a niche health food.

-

Now the downsides: contains some anti-nutrients. Phytates may reduce mineral uptake somewhat. Can be rough on some people digestively. Not everyone handles legumes equally well. Needs some cooking effort. A small practical friction versus more convenient foods.

-

Lentils has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when you want fibre, protein, and actual meal utility together.

-

A tier.
