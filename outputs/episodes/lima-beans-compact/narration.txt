Lima Beans!

-

Ranked!

-

0.7g of fat. Saturated fat, polyunsaturated fat, and omega3 are doing most of the work. For a legume, the whole appeal is cheap, filling nutritional payoff.

-

63.4g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For legumes, the carbs look much better when fibre and protein are both backing them up.

-

21.5g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein support is a big part of what makes legumes worth it.

-

Outside of that, the profile is pretty quiet.

-

Magnesium at 32% daily value, iron at 25% daily value, potassium at 27% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

-

The upsides first: filling for the calories. Legumes usually earn a lot of practical value from satiety. Does a lot of jobs at once. Protein, fibre, and meal utility are why legumes stay valuable. Fibre-forward category identity. Legumes are supposed to bring stable carbs and fibre.

-

On the weaker side: protein quality still has a ceiling. Good, but not meat or dairy level. Can take planning or prep. That is a small but real friction cost. Prep time can still be a barrier. Dry legumes especially ask more from the cook.

-

Lima Beans has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when you want fibre, protein, and actual meal utility together.

-

S tier.
