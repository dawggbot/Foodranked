Macadamia Nuts!

-

Ranked!

-

75.8g of fat. Saturated fat is a major pressure point. For nuts, fat quality has to justify the calorie density.

-

13.8g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For a nut, you need enough upside to make the density worth it.

-

7.9g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. Protein is support here, not the whole argument.

-

Everything else is kind of whatever.

-

Magnesium at 31% daily value, zinc at 13% daily value, iron at 20% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

-

On the plus side: holds up as a better snack default than most packaged options. That real-world swap value counts. Texture helps them feel satisfying. That can make moderation easier than with softer snack foods. Still a recognisable whole-food option. That keeps them cleaner than most processed snacks.

-

The drawbacks next: protein is useful but not elite. Nuts help, but they are not protein stars. Calorie density is very high. That makes portion control a real issue. Cost or allergy issues can limit use. Practical downsides still count.

-

Macadamia Nuts does have a few real-world upsides. But the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. Best only in small amounts when the calories stay under control.

-

D tier.
