Mango ranked.

fat is 0.4g. the fat quality is doing a lot of the work here. for a fruit, the cleanest wins are low sugar and decent fibre.

carbs is 15g. the sugar load matters more than you would want. for fruit, the real question is whether the sweetness stays under control.

protein is 0.8g. protein quality is a real strength here. for a fruit, the best ones stay sweet without going off the rails.

vitamin c at 40% DV, vitamin a at 12% DV stand out most. that is only mild support overall.

minerals are basically not part of the case here.

on the plus side. hydrating whole-fruit package because water and whole-food structure make it easier to use well than juice or sweets. easy to use as a whole food because simple, recognisable, and realistic for everyday eating. brings a clean fruit identity because it still contributes something valuable even when it is not elite.

now the downsides. protein contribution is tiny because that is normal for fruit, but it still limits nutritional breadth. sugar can climb fast because a lot of fruits look healthy but still lean sweet. satiety is not especially strong because fruit often needs help from the rest of the meal.

hydrating whole-fruit package and easy to use as a whole food help mango. but protein contribution is tiny and sugar can climb fast drag it down hard. best only when the convenience or taste matters more than the nutrition return.

C tier.
