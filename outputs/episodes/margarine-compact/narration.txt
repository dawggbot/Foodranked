The upsides first: easy and convenient to use. Simple practical upside. Often inexpensive. A practical reason it still exists in real kitchens. Very familiar product. People know what it is and how to use it.

-

On the weaker side: processing is a major category negative. This is the core reason margarine makes a clean weak anchor in this category. Ingredient simplicity is poor. Much weaker trust and wholefood fit than stronger oils/fats anchors. Quality varies wildly by formulation. That makes the category fit feel unstable and less trustworthy.

-

Margarine does have a few real-world upsides. But the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. Best only in small amounts when the cooking job matters more than the nutrition case.
