Pros first: easy protein delivery for normal diets. Dairy gets real credit for convenience. Easy to fit into familiar meals. That lowers friction for regular use. Slots into meals very easily. Practical versatility matters a lot for dairy.

-

On the weaker side: processing changes the category a lot. The label alone does not tell the whole story. Fat quality varies wildly across dairy. Some dairy is clean, some really is not. Some versions get dense fast on fat, sodium, or both. The category swings a lot by format.

Skimmed Milk has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when the protein payoff or fermentation angle outweighs the fat and sodium tradeoffs.
