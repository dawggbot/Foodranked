pros first: useful staple energy source because that matters because grains are often judged by how practical they are in real diets. works as a meal anchor because a lot of value comes from being easy to build around. usually affordable and scalable because practicality matters for staple foods.

now the downsides: protein quality is limited because even the better grains usually need pairing. carb load is still the main burden because that is the category risk no grain fully escapes. quality can drop fast with processing because the grain category punishes refinement pretty hard.
