Millet ranked.

4.2g of fat. omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. fat is basically not the story here.

72.9g of carbs. fibre is doing a lot of the work here. for grains, the carb quality matters much more than the raw number.

11g of protein. bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. protein helps, but not enough if the carb side is weak.

the rest is pretty lackluster.

magnesium at 29% daily value, iron at 17% daily value, zinc at 14% daily value stand out most. useful, but still pretty limited.

pros first: works as a meal anchor because a lot of value comes from being easy to build around. can keep meals cheap and steady because that is a genuine category strength. easy to batch cook at scale because that practicality is part of why grains matter.

now the downsides: needs strong pairings to feel complete because the grain rarely finishes the nutrition job alone. carb load is still the main burden because that is the category risk no grain fully escapes. easy to overshoot portions because staple carbs can pile up fast without much friction.

Millet does have some solid upside. but the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. best when you want a staple carb that actually brings something useful with it.

B tier.
