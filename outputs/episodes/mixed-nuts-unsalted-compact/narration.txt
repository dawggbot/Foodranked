Ranked!

54g of fat. Saturated fat, omega3, and polyunsaturated fat are doing most of the work. For nuts, fat quality has to justify the calorie density.

On the plus side: texture helps them feel satisfying. That can make moderation easier than with softer snack foods. Holds up as a better snack default than most packaged options. That real-world swap value counts. Fat and texture help satiety. They can hold up better than many snack foods.

-

On the weaker side: good versions can get expensive. That affects how realistic frequent use is. Cost or allergy issues can limit use. Practical downsides still count. Protein is useful but not elite. Nuts help, but they are not protein stars.

-

Mixed Nuts (Unsalted) does have a few real-world upsides. But the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. Best only in small amounts when the calories stay under control.
