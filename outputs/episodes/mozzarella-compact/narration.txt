Ranked!

17g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For dairy, the fat side can either add richness or drag the whole thing down.

-

3.1g of carbs. Sugar and glycemic index matter most here. For dairy, the question is whether the payoff is worth the baggage.

-

28g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Useful protein can rescue a lot of weaker traits here.

-

Vitamin b12 at 28% daily value, vitamin a at 12% daily value stand out most. That is only mild support overall.

-

Calcium at 51% daily value, zinc at 19% daily value stand out most. That gives this food real mineral support.

-

The upsides first: can be a very convenient protein vehicle. Convenience is one of dairy’s strongest practical advantages. Easy to fit into familiar meals. That lowers friction for regular use. Clear calcium identity. That is a core category strength.

-

On the weaker side: fat quality varies wildly across dairy. Some dairy is clean, some really is not. Not everyone tolerates dairy well. That is a real use-case limitation. Tolerance varies a lot person to person. A category strength is less useful if the food does not agree with you.

-

Mozzarella does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best when the protein payoff or fermentation angle outweighs the fat and sodium tradeoffs.

B tier.
