Navy Beans!

-

Ranked!

-

1.5g of fat. Saturated fat, omega3, and polyunsaturated fat are doing most of the work. For a legume, that is a really good sign.

-

61.3g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For legumes, the carbs look much better when fibre and protein are both backing them up.

-

22.3g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein support is a big part of what makes legumes worth it.

-

Everything else is lackluster.

-

Magnesium at 47% daily value, iron at 29% daily value, potassium at 39% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

-

The upsides first: cheap staple with real payoff. This matters because a food is stronger when people can actually use it often. Fibre-forward category identity. Legumes are supposed to bring stable carbs and fibre. One of the cheaper ways to buy useful nutrition. That matters for real diets, not just theory.

-

The drawbacks next: can be rough on digestion for some people. Practical tolerance still matters. Can take planning or prep. That is a small but real friction cost. Prep time can still be a barrier. Dry legumes especially ask more from the cook.

-

Navy Beans has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when you want fibre, protein, and actual meal utility together.

-

S tier.
