Nigella Seeds!

Ranked!

22.3g of fat. Saturated fat, omega3, and polyunsaturated fat are doing most of the work. For seeds, the fat profile is one of the biggest reasons they earn their place.

44.2g of carbs. Starch is doing most of the heavy lifting here. For a seed, this is a very strong sign.

Vitamin e at 18% daily value stands out most. That is barely moving the needle.

-

Magnesium at 92% daily value, iron at 55% daily value, calcium at 30% daily value stand out most. That gives this food real mineral support.

-

On the plus side: usually brings decent fat support. That is one of the category strengths. Often brings dense micronutrient support. That is part of what makes the category interesting. Easy to sprinkle into meals. That practical versatility matters.

-

The drawbacks next: paper scores can overstate realistic serving sizes. People often eat far less than 100 grams. Real serving sizes are often small. Some strong paper numbers are hard to cash out in practice. Texture or prep can be a barrier. Not everyone uses seeds consistently.

-

Nigella Seeds does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best as a support food that boosts meals instead of carrying them alone.
