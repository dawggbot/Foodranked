Oats!

-

Ranked!

-

6.9g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. Fat is basically not the story here.

-

66.3g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For grains, the carb quality matters much more than the raw number.

-

16.9g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein helps, but not enough if the carb side is weak.

-

Outside of that, the profile is pretty quiet.

-

Magnesium at 44% daily value, iron at 26% daily value, zinc at 36% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

-

Pros first: contains useful beta-glucans. Adds cardiovascular and satiety context beyond the base nutrient display. Easy to build meals around. Works well as a simple staple in many eating patterns. Can support digestive regularity. The fibre profile can be practically useful beyond the raw grams display.

-

On the weaker side: contains some anti-nutrients. That can slightly reduce mineral absorption. Healthiness depends on what you add to it. Sweet add-ons can change the whole picture. Not ideal for everyone digestively. Some people just do not tolerate it that well.

-

Oats has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when you want a staple carb that actually brings something useful with it.

-

A tier.
