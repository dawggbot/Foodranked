Pros first: makes meals feel more balanced. The category has real value beyond single-nutrient stats. Helps add volume without much calorie cost. That makes vegetables easier to use often. Good volume for the calories. That matters for satiety and general usefulness.

-

The drawbacks next: energy density is very low. Good for some use cases, but not enough on its own. Taste and texture can limit compliance. A strong paper profile still needs real-world repeatability. Rarely carries a meal by itself. That is normal here, but still part of the tradeoff.

-

Onion does have a few real-world upsides. But the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. Best only when you need a low-commitment add-on rather than a nutrient-dense anchor.
