Parmesan!

-

Ranked!

-

29g of fat. Saturated fat is a major pressure point. For dairy, the fat side can either add richness or drag the whole thing down.

-

4.1g of carbs. Sugar and glycemic index matter most here. For dairy, the question is whether the payoff is worth the baggage.

-

38g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Useful protein can rescue a lot of weaker traits here.

-

Vitamin b12 at 44% daily value, vitamin a at 26% daily value stand out most. That is only mild support overall.

-

Calcium at 92% daily value, zinc at 29% daily value, magnesium at 11% daily value stand out most. That is major mineral support.

-

The upsides first: clear calcium identity. That is a core category strength. Slots into meals very easily. Practical versatility matters a lot for dairy. Can be a very convenient protein vehicle. Convenience is one of dairy’s strongest practical advantages.

-

Now the downsides: very dense and easy to overshoot. The nutrient density is real, but so is the concentration. Fat quality varies wildly across dairy. Some dairy is clean, some really is not. Processing changes the category a lot. The label alone does not tell the whole story.

-

Parmesan does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best when the protein payoff or fermentation angle outweighs the fat and sodium tradeoffs.

-

B tier.
