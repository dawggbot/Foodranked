Parsnip!

-

Ranked!

-

0.3g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a tuber, carb behavior is everything.

-

18g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For tubers, the carb side decides whether the food feels stable or flimsy.

-

1.2g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. Protein is usually limited here, so the other sections have to carry more.

-

Vitamin c at 19% daily value stands out most. That is barely moving the needle.

-

Potassium at 11% daily value stands out most. Useful, but still pretty limited.

-

The upsides first: works across a lot of cooking styles. Utility counts for staple carbs. Easy comfort-food base with less baggage than junk food. That practical swap value matters. Can be quite filling. A useful practical strength when the preparation stays sensible.

-

On the weaker side: can go downhill fast when heavily processed. The category has a big preparation trap. Satiety payoff depends a lot on format. Some versions are much steadier than others. Protein support is weak. Tubers still need help from the rest of the meal.

-

Parsnip does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best when you want practical carbs and the rest of the profile is still reasonably clean.

-

B tier.
