Peanuts!

Ranked!

-

49.2g of fat. Polyunsaturated fat is one of the better parts of the profile. For nuts, fat quality has to justify the calorie density.

-

16.1g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For a nut, good fats have to justify the calorie density.

-

25.8g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein is support here, not the whole argument.

-

Vitamin e at 56% daily value stands out most. That gives this food real vitamin support.

-

Magnesium at 42% daily value, zinc at 28% daily value, iron at 25% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

-

On the plus side: unusually protein-forward for a nut. Tests whether nuts can handle an unusually high-protein member. Cheap and practical. Real-world advantage. Strong satiety. Useful practical upside.

-

The drawbacks next: allergy risk matters. Meaningful downside. Omega-3 support is weak. A big test of the current nuts philosophy. Awkward category identity. Botanically a legume, culturally a nut.

-

Peanuts does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best in small amounts where the fats and minerals matter more than sheer calories.

B tier.
