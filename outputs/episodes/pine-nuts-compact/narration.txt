Pine Nuts!

-

Ranked!

-

68.4g of fat. Polyunsaturated fat is one of the better parts of the profile. For nuts, fat quality has to justify the calorie density.

-

13.1g of carbs. Fibre is where things start to fall off. For a nut, fat quality matters a ton.

-

13.7g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein is support here, not the whole argument.

-

Everything else here is pretty flat.

-

Magnesium at 63% daily value, zinc at 46% daily value, iron at 31% daily value stand out most. That gives this food real mineral support.

-

On the plus side: holds up as a better snack default than most packaged options. That real-world swap value counts. Texture helps them feel satisfying. That can make moderation easier than with softer snack foods. Still a recognisable whole-food option. That keeps them cleaner than most processed snacks.

-

On the weaker side: good versions can get expensive. That affects how realistic frequent use is. Calorie density is very high. That makes portion control a real issue. Cost or allergy issues can limit use. Practical downsides still count.

-

Pine Nuts has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best in small amounts where the fats and minerals matter more than sheer calories.

A tier.
