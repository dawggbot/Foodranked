Vitamin c at 53% daily value stands out most. That gives this food real vitamin support.

Minerals are basically not part of the case here.

-

The upsides first: usually beats ultra-processed sweet snacks. That replacement value matters in real life. Easy to use as a whole food. Simple, recognisable, and realistic for everyday eating. Ingredient simplicity is hard to argue with. Whole-fruit clarity is part of the category appeal.

-

On the weaker side: can be easy to waste if timing is off. Perishability is a practical downside people actually feel. Easy to eat quickly when it is very sweet. That can blur the line between light and sugar-heavy portions. Sugar can climb fast. A lot of fruits look healthy but still lean sweet.

-

Pineapple does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best when you want sweetness that still earns its place nutritionally.
