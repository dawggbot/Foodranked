Pinto Beans!

-

Ranked!

-

1.2g of fat. Saturated fat, omega3, and polyunsaturated fat are doing most of the work. For a legume, fibre and practicality are supposed to carry the case.

-

62.6g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For legumes, the carbs look much better when fibre and protein are both backing them up.

-

21.4g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein support is a big part of what makes legumes worth it.

-

Everything else is kind of whatever.

-

Magnesium at 41% daily value, iron at 29% daily value, potassium at 30% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

-

Pros first: one of the cheaper ways to buy useful nutrition. That matters for real diets, not just theory. Fibre-forward category identity. Legumes are supposed to bring stable carbs and fibre. Filling for the calories. Legumes usually earn a lot of practical value from satiety.

-

The drawbacks next: can be rough on digestion for some people. Practical tolerance still matters. Texture is not universally loved. That can reduce how often people actually use them. Protein quality still has a ceiling. Good, but not meat or dairy level.

-

Pinto Beans has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when you want fibre, protein, and actual meal utility together.

S tier.
