Pistachios!

-

Ranked!

-

45.3g of fat. Saturated fat, omega3, and polyunsaturated fat are doing most of the work. For nuts, fat quality has to justify the calorie density.

-

27.2g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For a nut, fat quality matters a ton.

-

20.2g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein is support here, not the whole argument.

-

Vitamin e at 18% daily value stands out most. That is barely moving the needle.

-

Magnesium at 30% daily value, zinc at 16% daily value, iron at 22% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

-

On the plus side: fat and texture help satiety. They can hold up better than many snack foods. Texture helps them feel satisfying. That can make moderation easier than with softer snack foods. Portable, practical snack food. Nuts score real points in life because they are easy to use.

-

Now the downsides: protein is useful but not elite. Nuts help, but they are not protein stars. Portion control can get slippery fast. Dense foods punish casual handful math. Calorie density is very high. That makes portion control a real issue.

-

Pistachios does have a few real-world upsides. But the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. Best only in small amounts when the calories stay under control.

-

C tier.
