Plain Yogurt!

-

Ranked!

-

3.3g of fat. Cholesterol, saturated fat, and polyunsaturated fat are doing most of the work. For dairy, the fat side can either add richness or drag the whole thing down.

-

4.7g of carbs. Sugar and glycemic index matter most here. For dairy, the best versions keep the convenience without wrecking the fat profile.

-

3.5g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Useful protein can rescue a lot of weaker traits here.

-

Vitamin b12 at 18% daily value stands out most. That is barely moving the needle.

-

Calcium at 12% daily value stands out most. Useful, but still pretty limited.

-

Pros first: easy to fit into familiar meals. That lowers friction for regular use. Easy protein delivery for normal diets. Dairy gets real credit for convenience. Clear calcium identity. That is a core category strength.

-

On the weaker side: tolerance varies a lot person to person. A category strength is less useful if the food does not agree with you. Processing changes the category a lot. The label alone does not tell the whole story. Not everyone tolerates dairy well. That is a real use-case limitation.

-

Plain Yogurt has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when the protein payoff or fermentation angle outweighs the fat and sodium tradeoffs.

-

A tier.
