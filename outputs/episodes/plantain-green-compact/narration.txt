Green Plantain!

Ranked!

-

0.4g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a tuber, carb behavior is everything.

-

31.9g of carbs. The sugar load matters more than you would want. For tubers, the carb side decides whether the food feels stable or flimsy.

-

1.3g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. Protein is usually limited here, so the other sections have to carry more.

-

Vitamin c at 20% daily value stands out most. That is only mild support overall.

-

Potassium at 14% daily value stands out most. Useful, but still pretty limited.

-

On the plus side: works across a lot of cooking styles. Utility counts for staple carbs. Easy comfort-food base with less baggage than junk food. That practical swap value matters. Can be quite filling. A useful practical strength when the preparation stays sensible.

-

Now the downsides: protein support is weak. Tubers still need help from the rest of the meal. Preparation changes outcomes a lot. What you do to a tuber can make or break it. Still a carb-heavy category. That is the core ceiling tubers have to manage.

-

Green Plantain does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best when you want practical carbs and the rest of the profile is still reasonably clean.

B tier.
