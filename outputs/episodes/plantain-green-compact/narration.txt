Green Plantain ranked.

0.4g of fat. omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. for a tuber, carb behavior is everything.

31.9g of carbs. the sugar load matters more than you would want. for tubers, the carb side decides whether the food feels stable or flimsy.

1.3g of protein. bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. protein is usually limited here, so the other sections have to carry more.

vitamin c at 20% daily value, vitamin a at 3% daily value stand out most. that is barely moving the needle.

minerals are basically not part of the case here.

on the plus side: practical staple carb source because tubers are often judged by how usable they are day to day. whole-food carb package because that matters because it is a cleaner baseline than heavily refined carbs. can be quite filling because a useful practical strength when the preparation stays sensible.

now the downsides: still a carb-heavy category because that is the core ceiling tubers have to manage. preparation changes outcomes a lot because what you do to a tuber can make or break it. protein support is weak because tubers still need help from the rest of the meal.

Green Plantain does have a few real-world upsides. but the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. best only when you want easy carbs and can accept the missing upside.

C tier.
