Air-Popped Popcorn!

-

Ranked!

-

4.5g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. Fat is basically not the story here.

-

78g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For grains, the carb quality matters much more than the raw number.

-

12.9g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein helps, but not enough if the carb side is weak.

-

The rest is pretty lackluster.

-

Magnesium at 36% daily value, iron at 17% daily value, zinc at 25% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

-

On the plus side: useful staple energy source. That matters because grains are often judged by how practical they are in real diets. Works as a meal anchor. A lot of value comes from being easy to build around. Can keep meals cheap and steady. That is a genuine category strength.

-

The drawbacks next: quality can drop fast with processing. The grain category punishes refinement pretty hard. Easy to overshoot portions. Staple carbs can pile up fast without much friction. Needs strong pairings to feel complete. The grain rarely finishes the nutrition job alone.

-

Air-Popped Popcorn has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when you want a staple carb that actually brings something useful with it.

-

A tier.
