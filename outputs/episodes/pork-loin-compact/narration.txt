On the plus side: high satiety protein source. That is a major real-world strength for meat. Can control hunger well in real meals. That is a major reason the category stays relevant. Complete protein delivery is reliable. The category is supposed to do this well.

-

On the weaker side: cost and storage can be a pain. Practical downsides still count. The outcome depends heavily on preparation. A good raw profile can still be cooked into a worse result. Fat quality can go sideways fast. Some meats are strong until the fat profile shows up.

-

Pork Loin does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best when you want efficient protein and can accept the category tradeoffs that come with it.
