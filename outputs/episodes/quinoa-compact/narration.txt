6.1g of fat. omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. fat is basically not the story here.

64.2g of carbs. fibre is doing a lot of the work here. for grains, the carb quality matters much more than the raw number.

14.1g of protein. bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. protein helps, but not enough if the carb side is weak.

vitamin e at 2% daily value stands out most. that is barely moving the needle.

magnesium at 49% daily value, iron at 25% daily value, zinc at 18% daily value stand out most. useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

the upsides first: unusually strong protein case for a grain because designed to expose whether grain proteins rescue too hard. good mineral support because better than many grains. strong healthy-grain reputation because very likely audience debate food.

the drawbacks next: GI is only decent because not elite on carb behaviour. prep quality matters because can be unpleasant if badly prepared. can be expensive vs staples because practical downside.

Quinoa does have some solid upside. but the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. best when you want a staple carb that actually brings something useful with it.
