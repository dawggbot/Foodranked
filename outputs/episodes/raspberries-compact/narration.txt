The upsides first: polyphenol content adds extra upside. That gives it a useful antioxidant angle beyond the visible sugar and fibre story. Contains useful polyphenols. Adds plant-compound value beyond the base nutrient display. Light for the volume. A practical advantage for a sweet food.

-

On the weaker side: quite perishable. Storage life is not amazing compared with sturdier fruits. Can be pricey. Cost can limit how practical it is as a staple fruit. Protein contribution is negligible. Not a real flaw for fruit, but still basically absent.

-

Raspberries has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when you want sweetness that still earns its place nutritionally.
