Rice Cakes!

-

Ranked!

-

3g of fat. Saturated fat is a major pressure point. Fat is basically not the story here.

-

81.5g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For grains, the carb quality matters much more than the raw number.

-

7g of protein. Protein amount is doing most of the work here. Protein helps, but not enough if the carb side is weak.

-

Outside of that, the profile is pretty quiet.

-

Magnesium at 13% daily value, iron at 7% daily value, zinc at 8% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still pretty limited.

-

The upsides first: portable and convenient. Very easy to use as a quick snack base. Easy to portion and carry. Simple packaging advantage. Works with many toppings. Practical flexibility for snack content.

-

On the weaker side: does not justify taking up your grain slot. Other grain foods usually bring much more actual nutritional value. Usually needs help to feel worthwhile. It often relies on toppings or pairings to become a decent snack. Not very filling for the calories. Can be unsatisfying unless built up with other foods.

-

Rice Cakes does have a few real-world upsides. But the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. Best only as a light snack base or texture food, not as a strong staple by itself.

-

D tier.
