Ricotta!

-

Ranked!

-

12g of fat. Saturated fat is a major pressure point. For dairy, the fat side can either add richness or drag the whole thing down.

-

3g of carbs. Sugar and glycemic index matter most here. For a dairy food, that is a strong sign.

-

11.3g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Useful protein can rescue a lot of weaker traits here.

-

Vitamin b12 at 13% daily value, vitamin a at 10% daily value stand out most. That is barely moving the needle.

-

Calcium at 21% daily value stands out most. Useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

-

The upsides first: easy to fit into familiar meals. That lowers friction for regular use. Clear calcium identity. That is a core category strength. Can be a very convenient protein vehicle. Convenience is one of dairy’s strongest practical advantages.

-

On the weaker side: not everyone tolerates dairy well. That is a real use-case limitation. Processing changes the category a lot. The label alone does not tell the whole story. Fat quality varies wildly across dairy. Some dairy is clean, some really is not.

-

Ricotta does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best when the protein payoff or fermentation angle outweighs the fat and sodium tradeoffs.

-

B tier.
