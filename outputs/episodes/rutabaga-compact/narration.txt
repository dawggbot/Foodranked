Rutabaga!

-

Ranked!

-

0.1g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a tuber, the question is whether the starch is behaving itself.

-

8.6g of carbs. Glycemic index is where this starts to get messy. For tubers, the carb side decides whether the food feels stable or flimsy.

-

1.1g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. Protein is usually limited here, so the other sections have to carry more.

-

Vitamin c at 42% daily value stands out most. That gives this food real vitamin support.

-

Minerals are basically not part of the case here.

-

The upsides first: can be quite filling. A useful practical strength when the preparation stays sensible. Practical staple carb source. Tubers are often judged by how usable they are day to day. Whole-food carb package. That matters because it is a cleaner baseline than heavily refined carbs.

-

On the weaker side: satiety payoff depends a lot on format. Some versions are much steadier than others. Still a carb-heavy category. That is the core ceiling tubers have to manage. Protein support is weak. Tubers still need help from the rest of the meal.

-

Rutabaga does have a few real-world upsides. But the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. Best only when you want easy carbs and can accept the missing upside.

-

D tier.
