Rye!

-

Ranked!

-

1.6g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. Fat is basically not the story here.

-

75.9g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For grains, the carb quality matters much more than the raw number.

-

10.3g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein helps, but not enough if the carb side is weak.

-

The rest of the section is nothing special.

-

Magnesium at 31% daily value, iron at 15% daily value, zinc at 18% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

-

The upsides first: can keep meals cheap and steady. That is a genuine category strength. Usually affordable and scalable. Practicality matters for staple foods. Easy to batch cook at scale. That practicality is part of why grains matter.

-

On the weaker side: protein quality is limited. Even the better grains usually need pairing. Quality can drop fast with processing. The grain category punishes refinement pretty hard. Needs strong pairings to feel complete. The grain rarely finishes the nutrition job alone.

-

Rye has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when you want a staple carb that actually brings something useful with it.

-

A tier.
