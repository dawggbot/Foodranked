Sesame Seeds!

-

Ranked!

-

49.7g of fat. Saturated fat is a major pressure point. For seeds, the fat profile is one of the biggest reasons they earn their place.

-

23.4g of carbs. Starch is doing most of the heavy lifting here. For a seed, this is a very strong sign.

-

17.7g of protein. Essential amino acid support is one of the better parts here. Protein is a bonus, but not enough by itself.

-

Everything else here is pretty flat.

-

Magnesium at 88% daily value, calcium at 98% daily value, iron at 81% daily value stand out most. That is major mineral support.

-

The upsides first: elite mineral density. The mineral payload is a major category strength beyond a normal seed baseline. Easy to use across lots of foods. Works well in savoury dishes, baking, and toppings. Strong wholefood seed identity. Feels like a very legitimate seed-category food rather than an edge case.

-

Now the downsides: contains some anti-nutrients. Phytates may reduce mineral uptake somewhat. Not suitable for everyone. Sesame allergy risk matters in practice. Saturated fat is not negligible. Still not a disaster, but it is less clean than the best fat-profile seeds.

-

Sesame Seeds does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best as a support food that boosts meals instead of carrying them alone.

-

B tier.
