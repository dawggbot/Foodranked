Unsweetened Soy Milk ranked.

1.8g of fat. polyunsaturated fat is one of the better parts of the profile. for a legume, this is exactly the kind of profile you want.

4g of carbs. fibre is where things start to fall off. for legumes, the carbs look much better when fibre and protein are both backing them up.

3.3g of protein. bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. protein support is a big part of what makes legumes worth it.

everything else here is pretty flat.

minerals are basically not part of the case here.

on the plus side: cheap staple with real payoff because this matters because a food is stronger when people can actually use it often. does a lot of jobs at once because protein, fibre, and meal utility are why legumes stay valuable. filling for the calories because legumes usually earn a lot of practical value from satiety.

now the downsides: dilution makes it less impressive than whole legumes because the legume identity is still there, but not in full strength. protein quality still has a ceiling because good, but not meat or dairy level. texture is not universally loved because that can reduce how often people actually use them.

Unsweetened Soy Milk does have some solid upside. but the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. best when you want fibre, protein, and actual meal utility together.

B tier.
