Soybeans ranked.

fat is 19.9g. omega 3 is doing serious work here. which is exactly what you want from a legume.

carbs is 30.2g. the fibre support does a lot of the heavy lifting. for legumes, the carb story matters less when fibre and protein are both backing it up.

protein is 36.5g. protein quality is clearly one of the selling points. legumes need protein support to feel worth the carbs.

vitamin c at 10% DV, vitamin e at 6% DV stand out most. that is not enough to move the verdict very far on its own.

magnesium at 70% DV, iron at 49% DV, zinc at 37% DV stand out most. that is useful, but still not a huge mineral edge.

pros first. extreme protein/mineral legume because tests whether legumes can handle a very non-typical high-fat, high-protein member. very low glycemic behaviour because strong category fit. many practical uses because broad food-system relevance.

the drawbacks next. anti-nutrients matter because preparation and form matter a lot. often consumed in processed forms because whole soybeans are not the only real-world form. can be rough on some people because practical downside.

Soybeans stands out for extreme protein/mineral legume and very low glycemic behaviour. but it also gets held back by anti-nutrients matter and often consumed in processed forms. Best when you want fibre, protein, and actual meal utility together.
