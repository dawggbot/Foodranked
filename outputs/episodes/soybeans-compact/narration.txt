Soybeans!

-

Ranked!

-

19.9g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. Which is exactly what you want from a legume.

-

30.2g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For legumes, the carbs look much better when fibre and protein are both backing them up.

-

36.5g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein support is a big part of what makes legumes worth it.

-

Vitamin c at 10% daily value stands out most. That is barely moving the needle.

-

Magnesium at 70% daily value, iron at 49% daily value, zinc at 37% daily value stand out most. That gives this food real mineral support.

-

Pros first: extreme protein/mineral legume. Tests whether legumes can handle a very non-typical high-fat, high-protein member. Very low glycemic behaviour. Strong category fit. Many practical uses. Broad food-system relevance.

-

The drawbacks next: anti-nutrients matter. Preparation and form matter a lot. Often consumed in processed forms. Whole soybeans are not the only real-world form. Can be rough on some people. Practical downside.

-

Soybeans has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when you want fibre, protein, and actual meal utility together.

-

S tier.
