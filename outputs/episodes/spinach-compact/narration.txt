Spinach!

-

Ranked!

-

0.4g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a vegetable, low downside and high payoff is what you want.

-

3.6g of carbs. Starch is doing most of the heavy lifting here. For vegetables, low downside is useful, but there still needs to be real payoff.

-

2.9g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. Protein is not the main pitch here, but extra support still matters.

-

Vitamin k at 402% daily value, vitamin a at 56% daily value, vitamin c at 31% daily value stand out most. That is major vitamin support.

-

Potassium at 12% daily value, magnesium at 19% daily value, iron at 15% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still pretty limited.

-

On the plus side: elite micronutrient density. This is exactly the kind of nutrient-packed vegetable the category is supposed to reward. Very light for the volume. A practical plus for a maintenance-and-repair food. Easy to use in lots of meals. Works raw, cooked, blended, and as a side or add-in.

-

Now the downsides: contains anti-nutrients. Oxalates can reduce mineral usefulness in some contexts. Volume shrinks a lot when cooked. Can make portions feel smaller than expected. Not useful as a fuel food. That is fine for vegetables, but it does limit some use cases.

-

Spinach has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when you want low downside and easy micronutrient support.

-

S tier.
