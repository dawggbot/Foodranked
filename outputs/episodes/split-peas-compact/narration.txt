Ranked!

1.2g of fat. Saturated fat, omega3, and polyunsaturated fat are doing most of the work. For a legume, fibre and practicality are supposed to carry the case.

-

63g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For legumes, the carbs look much better when fibre and protein are both backing them up.

Everything else is kind of whatever.

-

Magnesium at 32% daily value, iron at 26% daily value, zinc at 23% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

-

The upsides first: one of the cheaper ways to buy useful nutrition. That matters for real diets, not just theory. Fibre-forward category identity. Legumes are supposed to bring stable carbs and fibre. Filling for the calories. Legumes usually earn a lot of practical value from satiety.

-

On the weaker side: can be rough on digestion for some people. Practical tolerance still matters. Texture is not universally loved. That can reduce how often people actually use them. Can take planning or prep. That is a small but real friction cost.

-

Split Peas has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when you want fibre, protein, and actual meal utility together.
