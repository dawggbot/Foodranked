Sunflower Seeds!

-

Ranked!

-

51.5g of fat. Polyunsaturated fat is one of the better parts of the profile. For seeds, the fat profile is one of the biggest reasons they earn their place.

-

20g of carbs. Starch is doing most of the heavy lifting here. For a seed, the best ones punch way above their size.

-

20.8g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein is a bonus, but not enough by itself.

-

Vitamin e at 175% daily value stands out most. That is major vitamin support.

-

Magnesium at 104% daily value, zinc at 45% daily value, iron at 29% daily value stand out most. That is major mineral support.

-

Pros first: usually brings decent fat support. That is one of the category strengths. Easy to sprinkle into meals. That practical versatility matters. Tiny food with serious nutrient density. Seeds often overperform for their size.

-

On the weaker side: texture or prep can be a barrier. Not everyone uses seeds consistently. Calorie density still stacks up fast. Tiny foods can still overshoot fast. Real serving sizes are often small. Some strong paper numbers are hard to cash out in practice.

-

Sunflower Seeds does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best as a support food that boosts meals instead of carrying them alone.

-

B tier.
