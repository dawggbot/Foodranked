Pros first: easy comfort-food base with less baggage than junk food. That practical swap value matters. Whole-food carb package. That matters because it is a cleaner baseline than heavily refined carbs. Practical staple carb source. Tubers are often judged by how usable they are day to day.

-

The drawbacks next: protein support is weak. Tubers still need help from the rest of the meal. Can go downhill fast when heavily processed. The category has a big preparation trap. Still a carb-heavy category. That is the core ceiling tubers have to manage.

-

Taro has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when you want practical carbs and the rest of the profile is still reasonably clean.
