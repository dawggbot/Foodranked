Tuna ranked.

0.8g of fat. saturated fat, cholesterol, and polyunsaturated fat are doing most of the work. for meats, fat quality matters a lot once the protein is already there.

0g of carbs. everything else here is pretty flat. in this category, carbs barely matter.

28g of protein. bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. strong protein is expected here, so the rest of the profile decides how high it climbs.

vitamin b12 at 43% daily value, vitamin d at 9% daily value stand out most. that is only mild support overall.

minerals are basically not part of the case here.

on the plus side: high satiety protein source because that is a major real-world strength for meat. complete protein delivery is reliable because the category is supposed to do this well. easy to centre a meal around because practical meal value matters.

now the downsides: fat quality can go sideways fast because some meats are strong until the fat profile shows up. cost and storage can be a pain because practical downsides still count. brings no fibre support because that leaves a nutrition gap the rest of the meal has to cover.

Tuna does have some solid upside. but the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. best when you want efficient protein and can accept the category tradeoffs that come with it.

B tier.
