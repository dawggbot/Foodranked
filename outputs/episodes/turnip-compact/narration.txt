Turnip!

-

Ranked!

-

0.1g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a tuber, glycemic behaviour matters a ton.

-

6.4g of carbs. Starch is doing most of the heavy lifting here. For tubers, the carb side decides whether the food feels stable or flimsy.

-

0.9g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. Protein is usually limited here, so the other sections have to carry more.

-

Vitamin c at 23% daily value stands out most. That is only mild support overall.

-

Minerals are basically not part of the case here.

-

On the plus side: works across a lot of cooking styles. Utility counts for staple carbs. Can be quite filling. A useful practical strength when the preparation stays sensible. Whole-food carb package. That matters because it is a cleaner baseline than heavily refined carbs.

-

On the weaker side: can go downhill fast when heavily processed. The category has a big preparation trap. Still a carb-heavy category. That is the core ceiling tubers have to manage. Satiety payoff depends a lot on format. Some versions are much steadier than others.

-

Turnip does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best when you want practical carbs and the rest of the profile is still reasonably clean.

-

B tier.
