Walnuts!

-

Ranked!

-

65.2g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For nuts, fat quality has to justify the calorie density.

-

13.7g of carbs. Starch is doing most of the heavy lifting here. For a nut, good fats have to justify the calorie density.

-

15.2g of protein. Essential amino acid support is one of the better parts here. Protein is support here, not the whole argument.

-

Everything else is fairly underwhelming.

-

Magnesium at 38% daily value, zinc at 21% daily value, iron at 16% daily value stand out most. Useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

-

On the plus side: contains useful polyphenols. Adds plant-compound value beyond the core table. Has strong healthy-fat branding appeal. A practical content advantage because viewers already associate walnuts with healthy fats. Can be very satiating. Dense fats and texture can help fullness in practice.

-

The drawbacks next: easy to overeat calories quickly. Energy density adds up fast. Contains some anti-nutrients. Phytates may reduce mineral uptake somewhat. Can go rancid if stored badly. A practical downside versus some sturdier staples.

-

Walnuts has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best in small amounts where the fats and minerals matter more than sheer calories.

-

A tier.
