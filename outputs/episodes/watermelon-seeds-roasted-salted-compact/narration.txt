Watermelon Seeds (Roasted & Salted)!

-

Ranked!

-

47.4g of fat. Saturated fat is a major pressure point. For seeds, the fat profile is one of the biggest reasons they earn their place.

-

15.3g of carbs. Starch is doing most of the heavy lifting here. For a seed, that is exactly what you want to see.

-

28.3g of protein. Bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. Protein is a bonus, but not enough by itself.

-

Everything else is kind of whatever.

-

Magnesium at 65% daily value, zinc at 54% daily value, iron at 38% daily value stand out most. That gives this food real mineral support.

-

Pros first: unusually protein-forward for a seed snack. That gives it some practical appeal versus lighter snack foods. Has clear crunchy snack appeal. Easy to understand and package as a snack food. Still brings some useful minerals. Not devoid of nutritional upside despite the weaker category fit.

-

The drawbacks next: salt load is a major practical negative. The roasted salted format brings a clear real-world downside. Weak fibre density for the seed category. Seeds are supposed to dominate on fibre more convincingly than this. Fat profile is rough for a seed-category food. Too much saturated fat and not enough omega-3 support for this category.

-

Watermelon Seeds (Roasted & Salted) does have a few real-world upsides. But the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. Best only as a supporting add-on rather than something to lean on heavily.

-

C tier.
