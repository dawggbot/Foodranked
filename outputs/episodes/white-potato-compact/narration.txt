White Potato!

-

Ranked!

-

0.1g of fat. Saturated fat and omega3 matter most here. For a tuber, this mostly lives or dies on carb behaviour.

-

17.5g of carbs. Glycemic index, fibre, and sugar are doing most of the work. For tubers, the carb side decides whether the food feels stable or flimsy.

-

2g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. Protein is usually limited here, so the other sections have to carry more.

-

Vitamin c at 22% daily value stands out most. That is only mild support overall.

-

Minerals are basically not part of the case here.

-

The upsides first: very practical staple food. Cheap, common, and easy to build meals around, which matters for a tuber category built on energy foods. Can be fairly filling. Especially when eaten in less processed forms. Brings some useful potassium. Not amazing, but enough to matter a bit in the category.

-

Now the downsides: glycemic behaviour is only middling. Not as clean as the best tubers on the carb-behaviour axis. Fibre is only modest. Decent, but not category-leading. Micronutrient return is fine rather than elite. Strong enough to be respectable, not enough to be exceptional.

-

White Potato does have some solid upside. But the tradeoffs keep it from climbing higher. Best when you want practical carbs and the rest of the profile is still reasonably clean.

-

B tier.
