White Rice ranked.

0.7g of fat. omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. fat is basically not the story here.

80g of carbs. glycemic index is where this starts to get messy. for grains, the carb quality matters much more than the raw number.

7.1g of protein. bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. protein helps, but not enough if the carb side is weak.

the rest is pretty lackluster.

zinc at 10% daily value stands out most. useful, but still pretty limited.

on the plus side: useful staple energy source because that matters because grains are often judged by how practical they are in real diets. works as a meal anchor because a lot of value comes from being easy to build around. usually affordable and scalable because practicality matters for staple foods.

now the downsides: protein quality is limited because even the better grains usually need pairing. carb load is still the main burden because that is the category risk no grain fully escapes. quality can drop fast with processing because the grain category punishes refinement pretty hard.

White Rice does have a few real-world upsides. but the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. best only as a light snack base or texture food, not as a strong staple by itself.

C tier.
