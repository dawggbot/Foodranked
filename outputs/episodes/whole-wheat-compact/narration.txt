Whole Wheat ranked.

2.5g of fat. omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. fat is basically not the story here.

72g of carbs. fibre is doing a lot of the work here. for grains, the carb quality matters much more than the raw number.

13.2g of protein. bioavailability and amino acid quality are both strong. protein helps, but not enough if the carb side is weak.

outside of that, the profile is pretty quiet.

magnesium at 34% daily value, iron at 20% daily value, zinc at 20% daily value stand out most. useful, but still not a major mineral edge.

the upsides first: can keep meals cheap and steady because that is a genuine category strength. works as a meal anchor because a lot of value comes from being easy to build around. easy to batch cook at scale because that practicality is part of why grains matter.

now the downsides: carb load is still the main burden because that is the category risk no grain fully escapes. quality can drop fast with processing because the grain category punishes refinement pretty hard. needs strong pairings to feel complete because the grain rarely finishes the nutrition job alone.

Whole Wheat has a genuinely strong overall case. the practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. best when you want a staple carb that actually brings something useful with it.

A tier.
