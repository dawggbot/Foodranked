Yam!

Ranked!

-

0.2g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a tuber, this mostly lives or dies on carb behaviour.

-

27.9g of carbs. Fibre is doing a lot of the work here. For tubers, the carb side decides whether the food feels stable or flimsy.

-

1.5g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. Protein is usually limited here, so the other sections have to carry more.

-

Vitamin c at 29% daily value, vitamin a at 11% daily value stand out most. That is barely moving the needle.

-

Potassium at 19% daily value stands out most. Useful, but still pretty limited.

-

The upsides first: practical staple carb source. Tubers are often judged by how usable they are day to day. Can be quite filling. A useful practical strength when the preparation stays sensible. Whole-food carb package. That matters because it is a cleaner baseline than heavily refined carbs.

-

On the weaker side: satiety payoff depends a lot on format. Some versions are much steadier than others. Still a carb-heavy category. That is the core ceiling tubers have to manage. Can go downhill fast when heavily processed. The category has a big preparation trap.

-

Yam has a genuinely strong overall case. The practical downsides are real, but they do not outweigh the upside. Best when you want practical carbs and the rest of the profile is still reasonably clean.

-

A tier.
