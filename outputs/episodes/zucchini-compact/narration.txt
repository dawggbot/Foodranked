Zucchini!

-

Ranked!

-

0.3g of fat. Omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. For a vegetable, this is all about actual nutritional payoff.

-

3.1g of carbs. Glycemic index, fibre, and sugar are doing most of the work. For vegetables, low downside is useful, but there still needs to be real payoff.

-

1.2g of protein. Bioavailability is one of the best parts of the protein story. Protein is not the main pitch here, but extra support still matters.

-

Vitamin c at 20% daily value stands out most. That is only mild support overall.

-

Minerals are basically not part of the case here.

-

Pros first: good volume for the calories. That matters for satiety and general usefulness. Helps add volume without much calorie cost. That makes vegetables easier to use often. Easy to build meals around. A practical plus because good foods need realistic use cases.

-

Now the downsides: rarely carries a meal by itself. That is normal here, but still part of the tradeoff. Usually needs pairing to make a full meal. Most vegetables are not complete by themselves. Energy density is very low. Good for some use cases, but not enough on its own.

-

Zucchini does have a few real-world upsides. But the nutrition case is still weak and the downsides drag it down hard. Best only when you need a low-commitment add-on rather than a nutrient-dense anchor.

-

C tier.
