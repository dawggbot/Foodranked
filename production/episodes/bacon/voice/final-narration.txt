-
minerals are basically not part of the case here.
-
pros first:
it is highly palatable and appealing, which is a real-world reason people love it. it also works as a strong flavour booster, which makes it useful in meals even in smaller amounts. and it has strong comfort-food appeal, which makes it popular and instantly legible for content packaging.
-
the drawbacks next:
sodium is a major drawback because it is a central practical health issue for bacon. processing is another major negative, which becomes more important now that more processed foods are in scope. and it rarely improves a meal much on its own, because it usually works more as a flavour add-on than as a genuinely strong meat choice.
-
bacon gets some credit for being highly palatable and for working as a strong flavour booster. but sodium and processing drag it down hard. best only if convenience, budget, or taste matter more than getting the cleanest meat option.
