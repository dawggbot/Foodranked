RICE CAKES!
-
RANKED!
-
fat is 3g. omega 3 is doing a lot of the work here. but fat is basically not the story.
-
carbs is 81.5g. glycemic index is where this starts to get messy. for grains, carb quality matters much more than the raw number.
-
protein is 7g. bioavailability and amino acid quality are both decent. but protein does not do enough to rescue the weak carb side.
-
vitamin e at 1% daily value stands out most. that is barely moving the needle.
-
minerals are basically not part of the case here.
-
pros first:
it is portable and convenient, which makes it easy to use as a quick snack base. it is also easy to portion and carry, which is a simple packaging advantage. and it works with many toppings, which gives it practical flexibility.
-
on the weaker side:
it does not justify taking up your grain slot, because other grain foods usually bring much more nutritional value. it also usually needs help to feel worthwhile, because it often relies on toppings or pairings to become a decent snack. and it is not very filling for the calories, so it can feel unsatisfying unless it is built up with other foods.
-
rice cakes are easy to carry and easy to use. but they are weak for the category and usually need other foods to do the heavy lifting. best only as a light snack base or texture food, not as a strong staple by itself.
